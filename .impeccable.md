## Design Context

### Users
ROSE-SEIN serves breast cancer patients, caregivers, volunteers, moderators, and the association team. People use it when they need a calmer way to find validated information, stay in touch with trusted humans, and keep personal support logistics organized without the cognitive load of a generic medical portal.

### Brand Personality
Calm, protective, human. The interface should feel privacy-first, supportive, and non-clinical. It should reassure rather than medicalize, and it should feel like a digital sanctuary rather than an operational dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Soft editorial light mode with warm tonal separation, soft gradients reserved for primary actions, and ambient depth instead of heavy contrast. Typography should do most of the brand work. The signed-in experience should remain calm and minimal, with fewer competing actions and less “mobile SaaS shell” energy.

### Design Principles
1. Reduce cognitive load before adding capability.
2. Let typography and spacing carry hierarchy before cards and chrome do.
3. Keep privacy, trust, and emotional safety legible in the interaction model, not only in the copy.
4. Prefer progressive disclosure over showing every option at once.
5. Avoid trendy UI tells that make the product feel generic or AI-generated.
