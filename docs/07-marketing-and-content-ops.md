# ROSE-SEIN Marketing and Content Operations

## Brand Positioning

ROSE-SEIN should present itself as:

- calm
- trustworthy
- human
- dignified
- editorial rather than transactional

It should not sound:

- alarmist
- promotional
- overly clinical
- growth-hack driven

## Brand and Voice Rules

- reassuring, never patronizing
- informative, never cold
- gentle, never vague
- supportive, never manipulative
- French-first in patient-facing copy unless the product adds a formal multilingual model

## Content Pillars

The existing public website provides the current content pillars:

- breast anatomy and understanding
- diagnosis and treatment education
- living with breast cancer
- beauty and wellbeing
- nutrition and dietetics
- news and blog content
- association identity and partner visibility
- volunteer, partner, and donation calls to action

The app should preserve these pillars while gradually organizing them into:

- actualités
- soins de support
- parcours and practical guidance
- association communication

## Editorial Relationship Between Website and App

### Current model

- website is the public institutional source
- app is the early product experience

### Target model

- shared voice and vocabulary
- documented publishing ownership
- clear rule for which channel owns which content

Recommended split:

- website:
  - broader public awareness
  - institutional presence
  - partner and donation visibility
- app:
  - authenticated guidance
  - personalized support
  - event participation
  - secure conversations

## Lifecycle Messaging

### New visitor

- explain what ROSE-SEIN is
- establish trust quickly
- direct to the most helpful support path

### Newly registered user

- explain profile choices
- explain privacy and anonymity options clearly
- highlight messaging, content, and events

### Returning user

- highlight new validated content
- highlight relevant events
- highlight important conversation updates

## Campaign Framework

Recommended campaign families:

- awareness and association visibility
- workshop and event participation
- wellbeing and support resources
- volunteer and partner calls
- donation campaigns

## Content Governance

Every published content type should have a documented owner:

- editorial owner
- validator or reviewer
- publisher
- support or moderation contact where relevant

## Marketing Deliverables for Future Phases

- onboarding copy guide
- campaign calendar
- landing-page to app conversion strategy
- website and app content governance workflow
