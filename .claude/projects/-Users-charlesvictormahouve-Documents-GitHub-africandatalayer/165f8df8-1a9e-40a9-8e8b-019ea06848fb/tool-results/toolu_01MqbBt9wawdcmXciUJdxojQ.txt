    26→## 1. Fraud Scenario Catalog
    27→
    28→### 1.1 Data Fabrication
    29→
    30→#### F-FAB-01: Fabricated POI (Phantom Location)
    31→
    32→| Field | Detail |
    33→|---|---|
    34→| **Description** | Contributor submits a POI (pharmacy, fuel station, mobile money kiosk) that does not physically exist. They fill in plausible details (name, hours, provider list) but the business is fictional. |
    35→| **Attacker Motivation** | Earn XP (5 points per CREATE_EVENT via `BASE_EVENT_XP` in `api/submissions/index.ts:59`) and climb the leaderboard without doing fieldwork. |
    36→| **Attack Method** | Take a photo of a random storefront or blank wall. Submit with a valid GPS location inside the Bonamoussadi geofence (`shared/geofence.ts` bounds: 4.0755-4.0999 lat, 9.7185-9.76 lng). Fill required fields: name + isOpenNow (pharmacy), providers (mobile_money), name + hasFuelAvailable (fuel_station) per `CREATE_REQUIRED_FIELDS` in `pointProjection.ts:19-23`. |
    37→| **Detection Difficulty** | High. The current system only validates that required fields are present and location is within bounds. There is no ground-truth cross-reference or duplicate detection. |
    38→| **Business Impact** | Critical. Phantom POIs corrupt the dataset. Downstream consumers (fintech apps, logistics platforms, health services) route users to nonexistent locations, destroying platform credibility. |
    39→
    40→#### F-FAB-02: Recycled / Copied Photos
    41→
    42→| Field | Detail |
    43→|---|---|
    44→| **Description** | Contributor reuses the same photo across multiple submissions, or downloads photos from the internet (Google Maps Street View, social media). |
    45→| **Attacker Motivation** | Avoid fieldwork while farming XP. A single field trip yields photos reused across dozens of fabricated submissions. |
    46→| **Attack Method** | Take one photo on-site, then submit it as evidence for multiple different POIs by changing the location coordinates slightly. Alternatively, download storefront images from Google Maps. |
    47→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. The current system extracts EXIF metadata (`submissionFraud.ts:95-127`) including GPS, capture timestamp, device make/model. Photos reused across submissions would share identical EXIF data. However, stripped EXIF (common in screenshots/downloads) bypasses this entirely. |
    48→| **Business Impact** | High. Multiple fake POIs backed by "evidence" that appears legitimate at casual inspection. |
    49→
    50→#### F-FAB-03: AI-Generated / Synthetic Data
    51→
    52→| Field | Detail |
    53→|---|---|
    54→| **Description** | Contributor uses AI image generation to create realistic storefront photos, or uses LLMs to generate plausible business details. |
    55→| **Attacker Motivation** | Scale fabrication without any physical presence. |
    56→| **Attack Method** | Generate pharmacy/kiosk storefront images using diffusion models. These images have no EXIF data (GPS, device info all null). Submit with fabricated coordinates within the geofence. |
    57→| **Detection Difficulty** | High. AI-generated images lack EXIF metadata, but so do many legitimate photos from feature phones. The current `extractPhotoMetadata` function returns all nulls for such images -- identical to a legitimate low-end device submission. |
    58→| **Business Impact** | Critical. At scale, this could flood the dataset with convincing but entirely fictional POIs. |
    59→
    60→### 1.2 XP Gaming
    61→
    62→#### F-XP-01: Rapid-Fire Submissions
    63→
    64→| Field | Detail |
    65→|---|---|
    66→| **Description** | Contributor submits dozens of submissions in rapid succession to accumulate XP quickly. |
    67→| **Attacker Motivation** | Climb leaderboard (`api/leaderboard/index.ts` ranks by XP then contribution count). Top positions may unlock real rewards via the rewards catalog. |
    68→| **Attack Method** | Use the offline queue (`lib/client/offlineQueue.ts`) to batch-prepare submissions while "offline," then flush all at once via `flushOfflineQueue`. The queue has no rate limiting -- it simply iterates all pending items sequentially. Server-side, XP is awarded immediately on insertion: `profile.XP = (profile.XP ?? 0) + BASE_EVENT_XP` (`api/submissions/index.ts:679`). |
    69→| **Detection Difficulty** | Low. Temporal clustering is easily detectable -- many submissions from the same userId within a short window. |
    70→| **Business Impact** | Medium. Leaderboard pollution discourages legitimate contributors. XP inflation devalues the reward economy. |
    71→
    72→#### F-XP-02: Self-Enrichment Loop
    73→
    74→| Field | Detail |
    75→|---|---|
    76→| **Description** | Contributor creates a POI (CREATE_EVENT, +5 XP), then enriches the same POI repeatedly (ENRICH_EVENT, +5 XP each) by filling in each gap field individually across separate submissions. |
    77→| **Attacker Motivation** | Multiply XP from a single physical location. A pharmacy has 3 enrichable fields (openingHours, isOpenNow, isOnDuty per `ENRICHABLE_FIELDS`); a fuel station has 6 fields. One visit could yield 7 submissions = 35 XP. |
    78→| **Attack Method** | Submit CREATE_EVENT with minimal required fields. Then submit separate ENRICH_EVENTs for each gap. The system validates that each enrichment fills "at least one currently missing field" (`api/submissions/index.ts:558`) but does not penalize drip-feeding. |
    79→| **Detection Difficulty** | Low. Pattern is visible: same userId, same pointId, multiple ENRICH_EVENTs in a short period. |
    80→| **Business Impact** | Medium. Inflates contribution counts and XP. May or may not produce bad data (the enrichment data itself could be legitimate). |
    81→
    82→#### F-XP-03: Collusion Rings
    83→
    84→| Field | Detail |
    85→|---|---|
    86→| **Description** | A group of contributors coordinate to validate each other's fake submissions, or systematically enrich each other's POIs with fabricated data. |
    87→| **Attacker Motivation** | Collectively inflate XP to dominate leaderboard and claim rewards. |
    88→| **Attack Method** | User A creates a POI. User B enriches it. User C enriches it further. Reverse roles for the next POI. Submissions are timed to avoid velocity flags. |
    89→| **Detection Difficulty** | High. Individual submission patterns look normal. Detection requires graph analysis of user-to-user and user-to-point relationships. |
    90→| **Business Impact** | High. Coordinated fraud at scale corrupts both data quality and the incentive structure. |
    91→
    92→#### F-XP-04: Alt Account Farming
    93→
    94→| Field | Detail |
    95→|---|---|
    96→| **Description** | Single person registers multiple accounts and submits from all of them. |
    97→| **Attacker Motivation** | Multiply XP earnings, dominate multiple leaderboard slots, or circumvent per-account limits (if implemented). |
    98→| **Attack Method** | Register with different email addresses. The current auth system (`lib/auth.ts` -> Supabase auth) has no device-binding or phone verification. The `deviceId` from `lib/client/deviceProfile.ts` is stored in localStorage (`adl_device_id` key) and sent as `clientDevice.deviceId` in submissions, but is not validated server-side for uniqueness across accounts. |
    99→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. Same deviceId appearing across multiple userIds is a strong signal. Same IP, same device fingerprint (memory, CPU cores, user agent) across accounts also signals alt farming. |
   100→| **Business Impact** | High. Undermines the one-person-one-account assumption. Corrupts leaderboard integrity. |
   101→
   102→### 1.3 Location Spoofing
   103→
   104→#### F-LOC-01: GPS Spoofing App
   105→
   106→| Field | Detail |
   107→|---|---|
   108→| **Description** | Contributor uses a GPS spoofing app (Fake GPS, Mock Locations on Android) to submit from a fabricated location without being physically present. |
   109→| **Attacker Motivation** | Submit POIs in areas they have never visited. Claim to have surveyed distant neighborhoods. |
   110→| **Attack Method** | Enable mock locations on Android. Set GPS to coordinates within Bonamoussadi bounds. Submit a photo (possibly taken elsewhere). The `navigator.geolocation` API returns the spoofed coordinates. The current system compares submission GPS to photo EXIF GPS (`submissionFraud.ts:157-172`), but if the photo lacks EXIF GPS (common on feature phones), this check produces null. |
   111→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. Cross-referencing with IP geolocation helps -- the current system fetches IP location via `ipapi.co` (`api/submissions/index.ts:223-240`). A 50km IP-to-photo threshold (`IP_PHOTO_MATCH_KM = 50`) catches coarse mismatches. However, a VPN user in the same city defeats this. |
   112→| **Business Impact** | High. Allows remote fabrication of an entire neighborhood's data without physical presence. |
   113→
   114→#### F-LOC-02: VPN / Proxy IP Masking
   115→
   116→| Field | Detail |
   117→|---|---|
   118→| **Description** | Contributor uses a VPN with an exit node in Cameroon to mask their real IP location. |
   119→| **Attacker Motivation** | Submit from a different country while appearing to be in Douala. |
   120→| **Attack Method** | Connect to a Cameroon-based VPN server. The `x-vercel-forwarded-for` or `x-forwarded-for` header now shows a Cameroonian IP. The `fetchIpLocation` function resolves it to a Douala-area coordinate, passing the 50km IP match threshold. |
   121→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. VPN exit IPs are often in known ranges (data center ASNs). The current system does not check ASN type. |
   122→| **Business Impact** | Medium. Enables remote fraud but requires combining with other techniques (photo fraud, GPS spoofing) to create a complete fake submission. |
   123→
   124→#### F-LOC-03: EXIF Stripping / Manipulation
   125→
   126→| Field | Detail |
   127→|---|---|
   128→| **Description** | Contributor strips or modifies EXIF metadata from photos before submission. |
   129→| **Attacker Motivation** | Remove evidence of the photo being taken elsewhere, or inject false GPS coordinates into EXIF. |
   130→| **Attack Method** | Use exiftool or similar to strip all metadata (GPS, timestamp, device info all become null). Or inject Bonamoussadi GPS coordinates into a photo taken in another city. The current `extractPhotoMetadata` (`submissionFraud.ts:95-127`) trusts EXIF data at face value -- there is no tampering detection. |
   131→| **Detection Difficulty** | Hard (stripping), Medium (injection -- injected EXIF often has telltale inconsistencies like impossible capture dates or mismatched software tags). |
   132→| **Business Impact** | High. Defeats the primary photo-location cross-validation mechanism. |
   133→
   134→### 1.4 Photo Fraud
   135→
   136→#### F-PHO-01: Stock Photos / Internet Images
   137→
   138→| Field | Detail |
   139→|---|---|
   140→| **Description** | Contributor submits stock photos or images downloaded from the internet as evidence of a POI visit. |
   141→| **Attacker Motivation** | Create fake submissions without visiting any location. |
   142→| **Attack Method** | Download a pharmacy storefront image from Google Images. Convert to base64, submit as `imageBase64`. The image will have no EXIF data (stripped by web platforms). The current system uploads to Vercel Blob via `put()` (`api/submissions/index.ts:186-193`) without content analysis. |
   143→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium with tooling (reverse image search, perceptual hashing), High without. Currently there is zero image content analysis. |
   144→| **Business Impact** | Critical. Completely fabricated evidence backing fabricated POIs. |
   145→
   146→#### F-PHO-02: Screenshot Submissions
   147→
   148→| Field | Detail |
   149→|---|---|
   150→| **Description** | Contributor takes a screenshot of Google Maps, another mapping app, or a previous ADL submission and submits it as their photo evidence. |
   151→| **Attacker Motivation** | "Prove" they visited a location using someone else's data. |
   152→| **Attack Method** | Screenshot a Google Maps storefront photo. The screenshot has the device's EXIF (capture time = now, GPS = current location if enabled) but shows a different location's content. The current system checks GPS match but not image content. |
   153→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. Screenshots have distinctive characteristics: exact screen resolution dimensions, UI overlays, navigation bars. Image dimension analysis could flag these. |
   154→| **Business Impact** | High. Creates plausible-looking but derivative evidence. |
   155→
   156→#### F-PHO-03: Same Photo Across Multiple Submissions
   157→
   158→| Field | Detail |
   159→|---|---|
   160→| **Description** | Contributor uses the exact same photo file for multiple different POI submissions. |
   161→| **Attacker Motivation** | Minimize effort while maximizing XP from fabricated POIs. |
   162→| **Attack Method** | Submit identical base64 image data for multiple CREATE_EVENTs at different coordinates. The current system uploads each to Vercel Blob with a unique path (`submissions/${eventId}-${Date.now()}.${ext}`) but never compares image content across submissions. |
   163→| **Detection Difficulty** | Low (exact match via hash), Medium (near-duplicate with minor edits). Currently not implemented. |
   164→| **Business Impact** | High. Clear indicator of fabrication -- a unique storefront photo should correspond to a unique physical location. |
   165→
   166→### 1.5 Account Abuse
   167→
   168→#### F-ACC-01: Multi-Account Farming
   169→
   170→| Field | Detail |
   171→|---|---|
   172→| **Description** | Same person operates multiple accounts, distributing submissions across them to avoid per-account velocity detection. |
   173→| **Attacker Motivation** | Evade rate limits, dominate leaderboard with multiple entries, hedge against account bans. |
   174→| **Attack Method** | Register N accounts with different emails. Alternate submissions across accounts. The `deviceId` in `lib/client/deviceProfile.ts:94-116` is stored per-browser in localStorage -- clearing storage or using incognito generates a new ID. |
   175→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. Device fingerprint correlation (same `deviceMemoryGb`, `hardwareConcurrency`, `platform`, `userAgent` pattern) across accounts is a signal. Same IP submissions across different userIds is another. |
   176→| **Business Impact** | High. Multiplies fraud capacity per attacker. |
   177→
   178→#### F-ACC-02: Automated Bot Submissions
   179→
   180→| Field | Detail |
   181→|---|---|
   182→| **Description** | Attacker scripts the submission API directly, bypassing the web UI entirely. |
   183→| **Attacker Motivation** | Submit at massive scale. Hundreds of fabricated POIs per hour. |
   184→| **Attack Method** | Obtain auth token (the current system uses Supabase auth). Script POST requests to `/api/submissions/` with fabricated payloads. The `imageBase64` field accepts any valid base64-encoded image. No CAPTCHA, no proof-of-work, no browser fingerprint verification on the server side. The offline queue's `idempotencyKey` is client-generated (`crypto.randomUUID()`) and not validated server-side for format. |
   185→| **Detection Difficulty** | Low to Medium. Bots often have: no `clientDevice` data, consistent user agent strings, impossibly fast submission rates, programmatic patterns in field values. |
   186→| **Business Impact** | Critical. Can flood the database with thousands of fake POIs in minutes. |
   187→
   188→#### F-ACC-03: Credential Sharing
   189→
   190→| Field | Detail |
   191→|---|---|
   192→| **Description** | Multiple people share a single account to accumulate XP faster or to let unskilled users submit on behalf of a "leader." |
   193→| **Attacker Motivation** | Pool effort into a single leaderboard position for maximum reward. |
   194→| **Attack Method** | Share login credentials. Multiple devices submit under one userId. Different `deviceId` values, different IP addresses, different device fingerprints -- all for one account. |
   195→| **Detection Difficulty** | Medium. Multiple distinct device profiles for one userId, geographically dispersed simultaneous submissions. |
   196→| **Business Impact** | Medium. Data quality may be acceptable if all sharers are legitimate field agents, but it violates platform terms and inflates individual rankings. |
   197→
   198→### 1.6 Data Poisoning
   199→
   200→#### F-POI-01: Intentionally Wrong Data
   201→
   202→| Field | Detail |
   203→|---|---|
   204→| **Description** | Contributor submits real POIs but with deliberately incorrect details: wrong prices, wrong hours, wrong availability status. |
   205→| **Attacker Motivation** | Vandalism, competitor sabotage (marking a competitor's station as "no fuel available"), or simply carelessness incentivized by XP-per-submission rather than XP-per-accuracy. |
   206→| **Attack Method** | Visit a real fuel station. Submit a legitimate photo. But report `hasFuelAvailable: false` when fuel is available, or set `pricesByFuel.super` to a wildly incorrect value. The current system validates field presence (`listCreateMissingFields`) but not field plausibility. |
   207→| **Detection Difficulty** | Very High for subtle errors (off-by-10% price), Medium for extreme values (fuel price of 1 XAF or 100,000 XAF). |
   208→| **Business Impact** | Critical. Correct-looking submissions with wrong data are the hardest fraud type to detect and the most damaging to data consumers. |
   209→
   210→#### F-POI-02: Competitor Sabotage
   211→
   212→| Field | Detail |
   213→|---|---|
   214→| **Description** | A competitor registers as a contributor and systematically marks rival businesses as closed, out of stock, or unavailable. |
   215→| **Attacker Motivation** | Redirect customers away from competitors. |
   216→| **Attack Method** | Enrich rival POIs with `isOpenNow: false`, `hasFuelAvailable: false`, `hasMin50000XafAvailable: false`. Provide a legitimate-looking photo of the closed sign or simply a storefront image. |
   217→| **Detection Difficulty** | High. Requires detecting that a specific user disproportionately submits negative availability data for a subset of POIs. |
   218→| **Business Impact** | Critical. Directly harms businesses and platform trust. |

   222→## 2. Detection Rules & Signals
   223→
   224→### 2.1 Current Implementation Analysis
   225→
   226→The current fraud detection system in `lib/server/submissionFraud.ts` provides:
   227→
   228→**What it catches today:**
   229→
   230→1. **Photo-to-submission GPS mismatch**: Compares EXIF GPS coordinates from the photo against the submission's declared GPS location. Threshold: 1km (`DEFAULT_SUBMISSION_GPS_MATCH_THRESHOLD_KM`). If the photo was taken >1km from the declared location, the submission is rejected with HTTP 400 (`api/submissions/index.ts:504`).
   231→
   232→2. **Photo-to-IP GPS mismatch**: Compares EXIF GPS against IP-geolocated coordinates. Threshold: 50km (`IP_PHOTO_MATCH_KM`). Rejects if the photo location does not match IP location.
   233→
   234→3. **Metadata preservation**: Extracts and stores device make, model, capture timestamp, GPS coordinates from EXIF. This is stored in `details.fraudCheck` on each event for later forensic review.
   235→
   236→4. **Geofence enforcement**: Non-admin submissions must fall within Bonamoussadi bounds (`shared/geofence.ts`). Rejects submissions outside the bounding box.
   237→
   238→5. **Admin forensics dashboard**: The `view=admin_events` endpoint (`api/submissions/index.ts:418-424`) builds `AdminSubmissionEvent` objects that include fraud check data, user profiles, and photo metadata for manual review.
   239→
   240→**What it misses:**
   241→
   242→| Gap | Severity | Description |
   243→|---|---|---|
   244→| No velocity / rate limiting | Critical | A user can submit 100 events per minute. No per-user, per-device, or per-IP rate limiting exists. |
   245→| No photo deduplication | Critical | Same image can be submitted for multiple POIs. No perceptual hash or exact hash comparison. |
   246→| No user trust scoring | High | All users are treated equally regardless of history. A new account and a 6-month veteran get identical treatment. |
   247→| No temporal analysis | High | No check for impossible travel (submissions 50km apart within 5 minutes). |
   248→| No duplicate POI detection | High | Two CREATE_EVENTs at the same GPS coordinates for the same category are both accepted. |
   249→| No EXIF-absent penalty | Medium | Photos without EXIF metadata (stripped, screenshots, AI-generated) receive the same treatment as photos with full metadata. The `submissionGpsMatch` is simply `null`. |
   250→| No device fingerprint cross-referencing | Medium | The `clientDevice.deviceId` is stored but never queried across submissions or users. |
   251→| No image content analysis | Medium | No screenshot detection, no reverse image search, no AI-generated image detection. |
   252→| No enrichment pattern analysis | Medium | Self-enrichment loops (same user, same point, multiple enrichments) are unchecked. |
   253→| No IP reputation checking | Medium | No ASN/datacenter detection for VPN/proxy identification. |
   254→| No field plausibility validation | High | Fuel price of 0 XAF or 1,000,000 XAF passes validation. |
   255→| No cross-user graph analysis | High | Collusion rings are invisible. |
   256→| XP awarded before validation | Critical | XP is added to profile immediately on submission (`api/submissions/index.ts:678-681`). There is no pending/review state. |
   257→
   258→### 2.2 Detection Rules by Scenario
   259→
   260→#### Rule R-VEL-01: Submission Velocity (addresses F-XP-01, F-ACC-02)
   261→
   262→**Signals:**
   263→- Count of submissions per userId in the last N minutes
   264→- Count of submissions per deviceId in the last N minutes
   265→- Count of submissions per IP address in the last N minutes
   266→
   267→**Detection Logic:**
   268→
   269→```sql
   270→-- Per-user velocity check (run before accepting submission)
   271→SELECT COUNT(*) AS recent_count
   272→FROM point_events
   273→WHERE user_id = :userId
   274→  AND created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '15 minutes';
   275→
   276→-- Flag if > 5 submissions in 15 min
   277→-- Auto-block if > 15 submissions in 15 min
   278→```
   279→
   280→```typescript
   281→// Server-side rate limiter (add to api/submissions/index.ts POST handler)
   282→const VELOCITY_WINDOW_MS = 15 * 60 * 1000; // 15 minutes
   283→const VELOCITY_FLAG_THRESHOLD = 5;
   284→const VELOCITY_BLOCK_THRESHOLD = 15;
   285→
   286→async function checkVelocity(userId: string): Promise<'ok' | 'flag' | 'block'> {
   287→  const recentEvents = await getRecentEventsByUser(userId, VELOCITY_WINDOW_MS);
   288→  if (recentEvents.length >= VELOCITY_BLOCK_THRESHOLD) return 'block';
   289→  if (recentEvents.length >= VELOCITY_FLAG_THRESHOLD) return 'flag';
   290→  return 'ok';
   291→}
   292→```
   293→
   294→**Thresholds:**
   295→- 5 submissions / 15 min -> flag for review, allow submission
   296→- 15 submissions / 15 min -> auto-block, return HTTP 429
   297→- 50 submissions / hour -> auto-suspend account
   298→
   299→**Confidence:** 90% for bot detection at block threshold. 60% at flag threshold (legitimate field agents doing a sweep could hit 5/15min).
   300→
   301→#### Rule R-DUP-01: Photo Deduplication (addresses F-PHO-03, F-FAB-02)
   302→
   303→**Signals:**
   304→- SHA-256 hash of the uploaded image buffer
   305→- Perceptual hash (pHash) of the image for near-duplicate detection
   306→
   307→**Detection Logic:**
   308→
   309→```typescript
   310→import { createHash } from 'crypto';
   311→
   312→function computeImageHash(imageBuffer: Buffer): string {
   313→  return createHash('sha256').update(imageBuffer).digest('hex');
   314→}
   315→
   316→// At submission time:
   317→const imageHash = computeImageHash(parsedPhoto.imageBuffer);
   318→const existingWithSameHash = await findSubmissionsByImageHash(imageHash);
   319→
   320→if (existingWithSameHash.length > 0) {
   321→  // Exact duplicate photo
   322→  const isSamePoint = existingWithSameHash.some(e => e.pointId === pointId);
   323→  if (!isSamePoint) {
   324→    // Same photo used for DIFFERENT points = strong fraud signal
   325→    return errorResponse("This photo has already been used for another submission", 400);
   326→  }
   327→}
   328→```
   329→
   330→**Thresholds:**
   331→- Exact hash match for different pointId -> auto-reject
   332→- Exact hash match for same pointId (re-enrichment) -> allow with flag
   333→- Perceptual hash similarity >95% for different pointId -> flag for review
   334→
   335→**Confidence:** 99% for exact duplicates. 80% for perceptual near-duplicates.
   336→
   337→#### Rule R-GEO-01: Impossible Travel (addresses F-LOC-01, F-ACC-03)
   338→
   339→**Signals:**
   340→- Sequential submissions from the same userId with GPS coordinates and timestamps
   341→- Calculated speed between consecutive submissions
   342→
   343→**Detection Logic:**
   344→
   345→```typescript
   346→async function checkImpossibleTravel(
   347→  userId: string,
   348→  currentLocation: SubmissionLocation,
   349→  currentTime: Date
   350→): Promise<'ok' | 'flag' | 'block'> {
   351→  const lastSubmission = await getLastSubmissionByUser(userId);
   352→  if (!lastSubmission) return 'ok';
   353→
   354→  const distanceKm = haversineKm(lastSubmission.location, currentLocation);
   355→  const timeHours = (currentTime.getTime() - new Date(lastSubmission.createdAt).getTime()) / 3600000;
   356→
   357→  if (timeHours <= 0) return 'block'; // Same timestamp, different location
   358→
   359→  const speedKmh = distanceKm / timeHours;
   360→
   361→  // In Douala: walking ~5km/h, moto-taxi ~30km/h, car ~60km/h
   362→  // Max reasonable: 80km/h in urban Cameroon
   363→  if (speedKmh > 200) return 'block';  // Physically impossible
   364→  if (speedKmh > 80) return 'flag';     // Suspicious
   365→  return 'ok';
   366→}
   367→```
   368→
   369→**Thresholds:**
   370→- Speed > 80 km/h between consecutive submissions -> flag
   371→- Speed > 200 km/h -> auto-block (physically impossible in Douala traffic)
   372→- Two submissions > 5km apart within 2 minutes -> auto-block
   373→
   374→**Confidence:** 95% at block threshold (200 km/h is not achievable on Douala roads). 70% at flag threshold (a moto-taxi on the highway could plausibly reach 80 km/h).
   375→
   376→#### Rule R-EXIF-01: Missing EXIF Metadata Penalty (addresses F-FAB-03, F-LOC-03, F-PHO-01)
   377→
   378→**Signals:**
   379→- Whether `extractPhotoMetadata` returns null for GPS, capturedAt, deviceMake, deviceModel
   380→- `clientDevice.isLowEnd` from device profile (mitigates false positives for feature phones)
   381→
   382→**Detection Logic:**
   383→
   384→```typescript
   385→function computeExifTrustScore(
   386→  extracted: ExtractedPhotoMetadata,
   387→  clientDevice: ClientDeviceInfo | null
   388→): number {
   389→  let score = 100;
   390→
   391→  if (!extracted.gps) score -= 30;
   392→  if (!extracted.capturedAt) score -= 20;
   393→  if (!extracted.deviceMake) score -= 15;
   394→  if (!extracted.deviceModel) score -= 15;
   395→
   396→  // Mitigate for known low-end devices that legitimately strip EXIF
   397→  if (clientDevice?.isLowEnd && score < 50) {
   398→    score = Math.min(score + 20, 50); // Partial recovery, but still flagged
   399→  }
   400→
   401→  return Math.max(0, score);
   402→}
   403→```
   404→
   405→**Thresholds:**
   406→- EXIF trust score >= 70 -> no action
   407→- EXIF trust score 40-69 -> flag, increase review priority
   408→- EXIF trust score < 40 -> flag + require secondary verification (second photo, manual review)
   409→
   410→**Confidence:** 50%. Many legitimate African-market phones produce images with minimal EXIF. This signal must always be combined with others.
   411→
   412→#### Rule R-PROX-01: Duplicate POI Detection (addresses F-FAB-01)
   413→
   414→**Signals:**
   415→- Haversine distance between new submission and existing points of the same category
   416→- Name similarity (Levenshtein distance or fuzzy match)
   417→
   418→**Detection Logic:**
   419→
   420→```typescript
   421→async function checkDuplicatePoi(
   422→  category: SubmissionCategory,
   423→  location: SubmissionLocation,
   424→  name: string,
   425→  existingPoints: ProjectedPoint[]
   426→): Promise<'ok' | 'flag' | 'block'> {
   427→  const sameCategory = existingPoints.filter(p => p.category === category);
   428→
   429→  for (const point of sameCategory) {
   430→    const distanceKm = haversineKm(location, point.location);
   431→
   432→    // Two pharmacies within 20 meters is almost certainly a duplicate
   433→    if (distanceKm < 0.02) return 'block';
   434→
   435→    // Within 50 meters with similar name -> flag
   436→    if (distanceKm < 0.05) {
   437→      const existingName = point.details?.name ?? '';
   438→      if (fuzzyNameMatch(name, existingName) > 0.7) return 'block';
   439→      return 'flag';
   440→    }
   441→
   442→    // Within 100 meters -> flag
   443→    if (distanceKm < 0.1) return 'flag';
   444→  }
   445→
   446→  return 'ok';
   447→}
   448→```
   449→
   450→**Thresholds:**
   451→- Same category within 20m -> auto-reject (suggest enrichment instead)
   452→- Same category within 50m with similar name -> auto-reject
   453→- Same category within 100m -> flag for review
   454→- Same category within 200m -> add to submission risk score
   455→
   456→**Confidence:** 85% at 20m/same-category. 70% at 50m. In dense urban Douala, multiple mobile money kiosks within 100m is legitimate, so the 100m threshold is a flag, not a block.
   457→
   458→#### Rule R-DEV-01: Device Fingerprint Cross-Reference (addresses F-ACC-01, F-XP-04)
   459→
   460→**Signals:**
   461→- `clientDevice.deviceId` from `lib/client/deviceProfile.ts`
   462→- `clientDevice.userAgent`, `deviceMemoryGb`, `hardwareConcurrency`, `platform`
   463→- EXIF `deviceMake`, `deviceModel` from photo metadata
   464→
   465→**Detection Logic:**
   466→
   467→```typescript
   468→async function checkDeviceCrossReference(
   469→  userId: string,
   470→  deviceId: string,
   471→  deviceFingerprint: string // hash of userAgent + memory + cpu + platform
   472→): Promise<'ok' | 'flag'> {
   473→  // Check if this deviceId has been used by other userIds
   474→  const userIdsForDevice = await getUserIdsByDeviceId(deviceId);
   475→  const otherUsers = userIdsForDevice.filter(id => id !== userId);
   476→
   477→  if (otherUsers.length > 0) return 'flag'; // Multiple accounts on same device
   478→
   479→  // Check if this device fingerprint matches other accounts
   480→  const userIdsForFingerprint = await getUserIdsByFingerprint(deviceFingerprint);
   481→  const fpOtherUsers = userIdsForFingerprint.filter(id => id !== userId);
   482→
   483→  if (fpOtherUsers.length >= 2) return 'flag'; // 3+ accounts with same fingerprint
   484→
   485→  return 'ok';
   486→}
   487→```
   488→
   489→**Thresholds:**
   490→- Same deviceId used by 2+ userIds -> flag both accounts
   491→- Same deviceId used by 3+ userIds -> auto-suspend newest accounts
   492→- Same device fingerprint across 3+ userIds -> flag for alt-account investigation
   493→
   494→**Confidence:** 75%. Shared devices are common in Africa (family members, cybercafe computers). The flag-not-block approach is intentional.
   495→
   496→#### Rule R-ENR-01: Self-Enrichment Pattern (addresses F-XP-02)
   497→
   498→**Signals:**
   499→- Count of ENRICH_EVENTs by the same userId on the same pointId
   500→- Time gap between CREATE_EVENT and ENRICH_EVENTs
   501→- Whether the creating user is also the enriching user
   502→
   503→**Detection Logic:**
   504→
   505→```typescript
   506→async function checkSelfEnrichment(
   507→  userId: string,
   508→  pointId: string,
   509→  existingEvents: PointEvent[]
   510→): Promise<{ score: number; isSelfEnrich: boolean }> {
   511→  const pointEvents = existingEvents.filter(e => e.pointId === pointId);
   512→  const createEvent = pointEvents.find(e => e.eventType === 'CREATE_EVENT');
   513→  const userEnrichments = pointEvents.filter(
   514→    e => e.eventType === 'ENRICH_EVENT' && e.userId.toLowerCase() === userId.toLowerCase()
   515→  );
   516→
   517→  const isSelfEnrich = createEvent?.userId.toLowerCase() === userId.toLowerCase();
   518→  let score = 0;
   519→
   520→  if (isSelfEnrich && userEnrichments.length >= 2) score += 30;
   521→  if (userEnrichments.length >= 3) score += 40;
   522→
   523→  // Check temporal clustering
   524→  if (createEvent && userEnrichments.length > 0) {
   525→    const createTime = new Date(createEvent.createdAt).getTime();
   526→    const enrichTimes = userEnrichments.map(e => new Date(e.createdAt).getTime());
   527→    const minGap = Math.min(...enrichTimes.map(t => Math.abs(t - createTime)));
   528→
   529→    if (minGap < 60000) score += 20; // Enriching within 1 minute of creation
   530→  }
   531→
   532→  return { score, isSelfEnrich };
   533→}
   534→```
   535→
   536→**Thresholds:**
   537→- Self-enrichment score >= 50 -> flag, cap XP for additional enrichments
   538→- Self-enrichment score >= 70 -> flag + require different user to enrich
   539→- Single user making 3+ enrichments on same point -> block further enrichments from this user on this point
   540→
   541→**Confidence:** 80%. Self-enrichment may be legitimate (revisiting a location), but the pattern is suspicious at scale.
   542→
   543→#### Rule R-VAL-01: Field Plausibility (addresses F-POI-01, F-POI-02)
   544→
   545→**Signals:**
   546→- Fuel prices vs. known market range
   547→- Opening hours format validity
   548→- Provider names against known provider list
   549→
   550→**Detection Logic:**
   551→
   552→```typescript
   553→const CAMEROON_FUEL_PRICE_RANGE_XAF = {
   554→  super: { min: 500, max: 1000 },    // ~630 XAF/L in 2026
   555→  gasoil: { min: 450, max: 900 },
   556→  kerosene: { min: 400, max: 800 },
   557→};
   558→
   559→const KNOWN_MOBILE_MONEY_PROVIDERS = [
   560→  'MTN Mobile Money', 'Orange Money', 'Express Union Mobile',
   561→  'YUP', 'Afriland First Bank', 'UBA'
   562→];
   563→
   564→function validateFuelPrice(fuelType: string, priceXaf: number): 'ok' | 'flag' | 'block' {
   565→  const range = CAMEROON_FUEL_PRICE_RANGE_XAF[fuelType];
   566→  if (!range) return 'ok';
   567→
   568→  if (priceXaf <= 0 || priceXaf > 10000) return 'block';  // Impossible
   569→  if (priceXaf < range.min * 0.5 || priceXaf > range.max * 2) return 'flag';  // Implausible
   570→  return 'ok';
   571→}
   572→```
   573→
   574→**Thresholds:**
   575→- Price <= 0 or > 10,000 XAF -> auto-reject
   576→- Price outside 50%-200% of known market range -> flag
   577→- Unknown mobile money provider not in known list -> flag (but allow, could be new entrant)
   578→- Availability status changed >3 times in 24h for same POI -> flag
   579→
   580→**Confidence:** 70% for price validation. Market prices shift, so ranges must be updated periodically.
   581→
   582→#### Rule R-IP-01: IP Reputation (addresses F-LOC-02, F-ACC-02)
   583→
   584→**Signals:**
   585→- IP address ASN type (residential, datacenter, VPN, Tor)
   586→- Multiple userIds submitting from same IP within short window
   587→
   588→**Detection Logic:**
   589→
   590→```typescript
   591→// Enhance the existing fetchIpLocation in api/submissions/index.ts
   592→interface EnhancedIpInfo {
   593→  location: SubmissionLocation | null;
   594→  asn: string | null;
   595→  org: string | null;
   596→  isDatacenter: boolean;
   597→  isVpn: boolean;
   598→  isTor: boolean;
   599→}
   600→
   601→async function fetchEnhancedIpInfo(ip: string): Promise<EnhancedIpInfo> {
   602→  // Use ipapi.co (already in use) or similar service
   603→  const res = await fetch(`https://ipapi.co/${ip}/json/`);
   604→  const data = await res.json();
   605→
   606→  const org = (data.org ?? '').toLowerCase();
   607→  const isDatacenter = org.includes('hosting') || org.includes('cloud') ||
   608→    org.includes('digitalocean') || org.includes('aws') || org.includes('azure');
   609→  const isVpn = org.includes('vpn') || org.includes('private internet') ||
   610→    org.includes('nordvpn') || org.includes('express');
   611→
   612→  return {
   613→    location: parseLocation(data),
   614→    asn: data.asn ?? null,
   615→    org: data.org ?? null,
   616→    isDatacenter,
   617→    isVpn,
   618→    isTor: data.is_tor === true,
   619→  };
   620→}
   621→```
   622→
   623→**Thresholds:**
   624→- Tor exit node -> auto-block submission
   625→- Known VPN/datacenter ASN -> flag, increase risk score by 25
   626→- 3+ different userIds from same IP in 1 hour -> flag all as potential alt accounts
   627→
   628→**Confidence:** 85% for Tor/datacenter. 60% for VPN (many legitimate users in Africa use VPNs for connectivity reasons).
   629→
   630→#### Rule R-GRA-01: Collusion Graph Analysis (addresses F-XP-03)
   631→
   632→**Signals:**
   633→- User-to-point bipartite graph: which users interact with which points
   634→- Reciprocal enrichment patterns: A enriches B's points AND B enriches A's points
   635→- Temporal coordination: submissions from different users on same points within narrow windows
   636→
   637→**Detection Logic:**
   638→
   639→```sql
   640→-- Find reciprocal enrichment pairs
   641→WITH enrichments AS (
   642→  SELECT
   643→    e.user_id AS enricher,
   644→    c.user_id AS creator,
   645→    e.point_id
   646→  FROM point_events e
   647→  JOIN point_events c ON e.point_id = c.point_id
   648→    AND c.event_type = 'CREATE_EVENT'
   649→  WHERE e.event_type = 'ENRICH_EVENT'
   650→    AND e.user_id != c.user_id
   651→),
   652→pairs AS (
   653→  SELECT
   654→    enricher AS user_a,
   655→    creator AS user_b,
   656→    COUNT(*) AS a_enriches_b
   657→  FROM enrichments
   658→  GROUP BY enricher, creator
   659→)
   660→SELECT
   661→  p1.user_a,
   662→  p1.user_b,
   663→  p1.a_enriches_b,
   664→  COALESCE(p2.a_enriches_b, 0) AS b_enriches_a
   665→FROM pairs p1
   666→LEFT JOIN pairs p2
   667→  ON p1.user_a = p2.user_b AND p1.user_b = p2.user_a
   668→WHERE p1.a_enriches_b >= 3
   669→  AND COALESCE(p2.a_enriches_b, 0) >= 3
   670→ORDER BY (p1.a_enriches_b + COALESCE(p2.a_enriches_b, 0)) DESC;
   671→```
   672→
   673→**Thresholds:**
   674→- Reciprocal enrichment between two users >= 3 times each -> flag both accounts
   675→- 3+ users all enriching each other's points >= 2 times -> escalate as collusion ring
   676→- Cluster of users with >80% of their enrichments going to other cluster members -> auto-flag all
   677→
   678→**Confidence:** 75%. Legitimate community behavior (neighbors validating each other's submissions) can look like collusion. Requires manual review.

   681→
   682→## 3. Risk Scoring Model
   683→
   684→### 3.1 Submission Risk Score (0-100)
   685→
   686→Every submission receives a composite risk score computed from five trust dimensions. Higher score = higher risk = more likely fraudulent.
   687→
   688→```
   689→SubmissionRiskScore = w_loc * LocationRisk
   690→                    + w_pho * PhotoRisk
   691→                    + w_tmp * TemporalRisk
   692→                    + w_usr * UserRisk
   693→                    + w_bhv * BehavioralRisk
   694→```
   695→
   696→#### Component Weights (Initial Calibration)
   697→
   698→| Component | Weight | Rationale |
   699→|---|---|---|
   700→| Location Trust (LocationRisk) | 0.25 | GPS/IP/EXIF triangulation is the strongest objective signal |
   701→| Photo Trust (PhotoRisk) | 0.25 | Photo evidence is the primary proof of physical presence |
   702→| Temporal Trust (TemporalRisk) | 0.15 | Velocity and timing patterns |
   703→| User Trust (UserRisk) | 0.20 | Account age, history, prior fraud flags |
   704→| Behavioral Trust (BehavioralRisk) | 0.15 | Submission patterns, enrichment behavior |
   705→
   706→#### 3.1.1 Location Risk (0-100)
   707→
   708→```typescript
   709→function computeLocationRisk(params: {
   710→  submissionLocation: SubmissionLocation | null;
   711→  photoExifGps: SubmissionLocation | null;
   712→  ipLocation: SubmissionLocation | null;
   713→  effectiveLocation: SubmissionLocation;
   714→}): number {
   715→  let risk = 0;
   716→
   717→  // No submission GPS provided (using fallback)
   718→  if (!params.submissionLocation) risk += 15;
   719→
   720→  // No EXIF GPS in photo
   721→  if (!params.photoExifGps) risk += 20;
   722→
   723→  // No IP location resolved
   724→  if (!params.ipLocation) risk += 10;
   725→
   726→  // Submission-to-photo GPS mismatch (if both present)
   727→  if (params.submissionLocation && params.photoExifGps) {
   728→    const dist = haversineKm(params.submissionLocation, params.photoExifGps);
   729→    if (dist > 1) risk += 40;       // Beyond 1km threshold
   730→    else if (dist > 0.5) risk += 15; // Marginal
   731→  }
   732→
   733→  // IP-to-effective location mismatch
   734→  if (params.ipLocation) {
   735→    const dist = haversineKm(params.ipLocation, params.effectiveLocation);
   736→    if (dist > 50) risk += 30;
   737→    else if (dist > 20) risk += 10;
   738→  }
   739→
   740→  return Math.min(100, risk);
   741→}
   742→```
   743→
   744→#### 3.1.2 Photo Risk (0-100)
   745→
   746→```typescript
   747→function computePhotoRisk(params: {
   748→  hasExifGps: boolean;
   749→  hasExifTimestamp: boolean;
   750→  hasDeviceInfo: boolean;
   751→  imageHash: string;
   752→  existingHashes: Set<string>;
   753→  imageDimensions: { width: number; height: number };
   754→  isLowEndDevice: boolean;
   755→}): number {
   756→  let risk = 0;
   757→
   758→  // Photo hash already exists for different point
   759→  if (params.existingHashes.has(params.imageHash)) risk += 80;
   760→
   761→  // Missing EXIF metadata
   762→  if (!params.hasExifGps) risk += 15;
   763→  if (!params.hasExifTimestamp) risk += 10;
   764→  if (!params.hasDeviceInfo) risk += 10;
   765→
   766→  // Screenshot dimensions (common phone screen resolutions)
   767→  const { width, height } = params.imageDimensions;
   768→  const isScreenshotRatio = (
   769→    (width === 1080 && height === 2400) || // Common Android screenshot
   770→    (width === 1170 && height === 2532) || // iPhone 12/13 screenshot
   771→    (width === 1284 && height === 2778)    // iPhone 12/13 Pro Max screenshot
   772→  );
   773→  if (isScreenshotRatio) risk += 15;
   774→
   775→  // Mitigate for low-end devices
   776→  if (params.isLowEndDevice && risk > 0 && risk <= 35) {
   777→    risk = Math.max(0, risk - 10);
   778→  }
   779→
   780→  return Math.min(100, risk);
   781→}
   782→```
   783→
   784→#### 3.1.3 Temporal Risk (0-100)
   785→
   786→```typescript
   787→function computeTemporalRisk(params: {
   788→  recentSubmissionCount15min: number;
   789→  recentSubmissionCount1hr: number;
   790→  lastSubmissionLocation: SubmissionLocation | null;
   791→  lastSubmissionTime: Date | null;
   792→  currentLocation: SubmissionLocation;
   793→  currentTime: Date;
   794→}): number {
   795→  let risk = 0;
   796→
   797→  // Velocity
   798→  if (params.recentSubmissionCount15min > 10) risk += 50;
   799→  else if (params.recentSubmissionCount15min > 5) risk += 25;
   800→  else if (params.recentSubmissionCount15min > 3) risk += 10;
   801→
   802→  // Impossible travel
   803→  if (params.lastSubmissionLocation && params.lastSubmissionTime) {
   804→    const distKm = haversineKm(params.lastSubmissionLocation, params.currentLocation);
   805→    const hoursElapsed = (params.currentTime.getTime() - params.lastSubmissionTime.getTime()) / 3600000;
   806→    if (hoursElapsed > 0) {
   807→      const speedKmh = distKm / hoursElapsed;
   808→      if (speedKmh > 200) risk += 50;
   809→      else if (speedKmh > 80) risk += 25;
   810→    }
   811→  }
   812→
   813→  // Late-night submissions (less likely to be legitimate fieldwork)
   814→  const hour = params.currentTime.getUTCHours() + 1; // UTC+1 for Cameroon
   815→  if (hour >= 23 || hour < 5) risk += 10;
   816→
   817→  return Math.min(100, risk);
   818→}
   819→```
   820→
   821→#### 3.1.4 User Risk (0-100)
   822→
   823→```typescript
   824→function computeUserRisk(params: {
   825→  accountAgeDays: number;
   826→  totalSubmissions: number;
   827→  priorFraudFlags: number;
   828→  priorRejections: number;
   829→  userTrustScore: number; // 0-100, see section 3.2
   830→}): number {
   831→  let risk = 0;
   832→
   833→  // New account penalty
   834→  if (params.accountAgeDays < 1) risk += 25;
   835→  else if (params.accountAgeDays < 7) risk += 15;
   836→  else if (params.accountAgeDays < 30) risk += 5;
   837→
   838→  // No submission history
   839→  if (params.totalSubmissions === 0) risk += 15;
   840→
   841→  // Prior fraud history
   842→  risk += Math.min(40, params.priorFraudFlags * 10);
   843→  risk += Math.min(20, params.priorRejections * 5);
   844→
   845→  // Inverse of user trust score
   846→  risk += Math.round((100 - params.userTrustScore) * 0.2);
   847→
   848→  return Math.min(100, risk);
   849→}
   850→```
   851→
   852→#### 3.1.5 Behavioral Risk (0-100)
   853→
   854→```typescript
   855→function computeBehavioralRisk(params: {
   856→  selfEnrichmentCount: number;
   857→  samePointEnrichments: number;
   858→  categoryDistribution: Map<string, number>; // how many of each category
   859→  totalSubmissions: number;
   860→  deviceIdSharedWithOtherUsers: boolean;
   861→  ipSharedWithOtherUsers: boolean;
   862→}): number {
   863→  let risk = 0;
   864→
   865→  // Self-enrichment pattern
   866→  if (params.selfEnrichmentCount > 5) risk += 30;
   867→  else if (params.selfEnrichmentCount > 2) risk += 15;
   868→
   869→  // Excessive enrichments on single point
   870→  if (params.samePointEnrichments > 3) risk += 25;
   871→
   872→  // Monoculture (only submitting one category could indicate targeted farming)
   873→  if (params.totalSubmissions > 10) {
   874→    const maxCategoryPct = Math.max(...params.categoryDistribution.values()) / params.totalSubmissions;
   875→    if (maxCategoryPct > 0.95) risk += 10;
   876→  }
   877→
   878→  // Device/IP sharing signals
   879→  if (params.deviceIdSharedWithOtherUsers) risk += 15;
   880→  if (params.ipSharedWithOtherUsers) risk += 10;
   881→
   882→  return Math.min(100, risk);
   883→}
   884→```
   885→
   886→### 3.2 User Trust Score (0-100)
   887→
   888→A long-term reputation score that builds with legitimate contributions and decays with fraud signals.
   889→
   890→```typescript
   891→interface UserTrustProfile {
   892→  userId: string;
   893→  trustScore: number;           // 0-100, starts at 50
   894→  totalApproved: number;        // Submissions that passed review
   895→  totalFlagged: number;         // Submissions that were flagged
   896→  totalRejected: number;        // Submissions rejected for fraud
   897→  consecutiveClean: number;     // Streak of unflagged submissions
   898→  lastFraudFlag: string | null; // ISO date
   899→  tier: 'new' | 'standard' | 'trusted' | 'elite' | 'suspended';
   900→}
   901→
   902→function updateTrustScore(profile: UserTrustProfile, event: 'approved' | 'flagged' | 'rejected'): void {
   903→  switch (event) {
   904→    case 'approved':
   905→      profile.totalApproved++;
   906→      profile.consecutiveClean++;
   907→      // Gradual increase: +1 per clean submission, +2 if on a streak
   908→      const bonus = profile.consecutiveClean > 10 ? 2 : 1;
   909→      profile.trustScore = Math.min(100, profile.trustScore + bonus);
   910→      break;
   911→
   912→    case 'flagged':
   913→      profile.totalFlagged++;
   914→      profile.consecutiveClean = 0;
   915→      // Moderate decrease
   916→      profile.trustScore = Math.max(0, profile.trustScore - 5);
   917→      profile.lastFraudFlag = new Date().toISOString();
   918→      break;
   919→
   920→    case 'rejected':
   921→      profile.totalRejected++;
   922→      profile.consecutiveClean = 0;
   923→      // Significant decrease
   924→      profile.trustScore = Math.max(0, profile.trustScore - 15);
   925→      profile.lastFraudFlag = new Date().toISOString();
   926→      break;
   927→  }
   928→
   929→  // Update tier
   930→  if (profile.trustScore >= 85 && profile.totalApproved >= 50) profile.tier = 'elite';
   931→  else if (profile.trustScore >= 70 && profile.totalApproved >= 20) profile.tier = 'trusted';
   932→  else if (profile.trustScore >= 30) profile.tier = 'standard';
   933→  else if (profile.trustScore >= 10) profile.tier = 'new';
   934→  else profile.tier = 'suspended';
   935→}
   936→```
   937→
   938→**Trust Tiers:**
   939→
   940→| Tier | Trust Score | Requirements | Privileges |
   941→|---|---|---|---|
   942→| New | 30-49 | Default for new accounts | All submissions reviewed. Max 10/day. |
   943→| Standard | 50-69 | 10+ approved submissions | Normal rate limits. Flagged only by rules. |
   944→| Trusted | 70-84 | 20+ approved, score >= 70 | Relaxed thresholds. Some submissions auto-approved. |
   945→| Elite | 85-100 | 50+ approved, score >= 85 | Can validate others' submissions. Highest auto-approve rate. |
   946→| Suspended | 0-9 | Triggered by fraud | All submissions blocked. Appeal required. |
   947→
   948→**Decay:** Trust score decays by 1 point per 30 days of inactivity to prevent dormant accounts from retaining high trust indefinitely.
   949→
   950→### 3.3 Score Thresholds and Actions
   951→
   952→| Composite Risk Score | Action | XP Treatment |
   953→|---|---|---|
   954→| 0-25 | **Auto-approve**. Submission accepted, XP awarded immediately. | Full XP (+5) |
   955→| 26-50 | **Soft review**. Submission accepted, XP awarded, added to review queue. Admin reviews within 48h. | Full XP, reversible |
   956→| 51-75 | **Hard review**. Submission accepted but XP held in escrow. Requires admin approval within 72h. | XP escrowed |
   957→| 76-100 | **Auto-reject**. Submission rejected with reason. User notified. | No XP |
   958→
   959→**Critical change from current behavior:** Currently XP is awarded immediately and unconditionally (`profile.XP = (profile.XP ?? 0) + BASE_EVENT_XP`). The proposed model introduces XP escrow for medium-high risk submissions.
   960→
   961→### 3.4 Weight Calibration Strategy
   962→
   963→Phase 1 (Weeks 1-4): Deploy scoring in **shadow mode**. Compute risk scores for all submissions but do not act on them. Log scores alongside submission data.
   964→
   965→Phase 2 (Weeks 5-8): Analyze shadow scores against manually labeled data. Admin team reviews a random sample of 100 submissions and labels each as legitimate/suspicious/fraudulent. Use labels to:
   966→- Identify which signals correlate most strongly with actual fraud
   967→- Adjust weights using logistic regression or simple gradient descent
   968→- Set thresholds that produce acceptable false positive rates (<5%)
   969→
   970→Phase 3 (Weeks 9-12): Enable enforcement mode with conservative thresholds (auto-reject only at 90+). Gradually lower thresholds as confidence grows.
   971→
   972→Phase 4 (Ongoing): Monthly recalibration using the previous month's manual review labels. Track precision/recall for each component.

   976→## 4. Response Playbooks
   977→
   978→### 4.1 Playbook P-FAB: Data Fabrication Response
   979→
   980→**Trigger:** Submission risk score > 75 with LocationRisk > 60 or PhotoRisk > 70.
   981→
   982→**Immediate Automated Response:**
   983→1. Reject the submission (HTTP 400 with fraud reason code)
   984→2. Do not award XP
   985→3. Log the rejection with full fraud check payload to `fraud_audit_log` table
   986→4. Increment user's `priorRejections` counter
   987→5. If user has 3+ rejections in 24 hours, auto-suspend account
   988→
   989→**Escalation Criteria:**
   990→- User has > 5 lifetime rejections -> escalate to admin queue
   991→- Rejection involves a POI that was previously approved (conflicting data) -> immediate admin review
   992→- Pattern of fabrication detected across multiple related submissions -> escalate as organized fraud
   993→
   994→**Investigation Steps:**
   995→1. Pull all submissions from the flagged userId (query `point_events` where `user_id = :flaggedId`)
   996→2. Cross-reference device fingerprints: check if `clientDevice.deviceId` appears under other userIds
   997→3. Review photo evidence: open each photo URL in admin dashboard, compare to Google Maps Street View for the GPS coordinates
   998→4. Check IP history: was this user submitting from consistent IPs (suggesting a real location) or varied datacenter IPs?
   999→5. Interview the user (if contactable): request they resubmit with a video or live photo
  1000→
  1001→**Resolution Options:**
  1002→
  1003→| Action | Criteria | Reversible? |
  1004→|---|---|---|
  1005→| Warning | First offense, low confidence in fraud determination | Yes |
  1006→| XP clawback | Confirmed fabrication, remove XP for fraudulent submissions | Yes via appeal |
  1007→| Submission deletion | Confirmed fabrication, admin calls DELETE on event ID | No (soft delete with audit trail) |
  1008→| Account suspension (7 days) | 3+ confirmed fabrications | Yes via appeal |
  1009→| Permanent ban | Repeated confirmed fabrication after suspension | Via appeal to senior admin |
  1010→
  1011→**Appeal Process:**
  1012→1. User submits appeal via in-app form or email
  1013→2. Appeal reviewed by different admin than the one who flagged
  1014→3. If evidence is ambiguous, user is asked to provide additional proof (return to location, take timestamped video)
  1015→4. Appeal decision rendered within 5 business days
  1016→5. If overturned, XP restored and trust score partially recovered (+10)
  1017→
  1018→**Evidence Preservation:**
  1019→- All photo URLs are retained in Vercel Blob (do not delete blob storage even if submission event is deleted)
  1020→- Fraud check payload preserved in `details.fraudCheck` on the event
  1021→- Admin actions logged with admin userId, timestamp, and reason in `fraud_audit_log`
  1022→- IP addresses and device fingerprints retained for 90 days for forensic analysis
  1023→
  1024→### 4.2 Playbook P-XPG: XP Gaming Response
  1025→
  1026→**Trigger:** TemporalRisk > 50 or BehavioralRisk > 50, or self-enrichment pattern detected.
  1027→
  1028→**Immediate Automated Response:**
  1029→1. For velocity violations: rate-limit the user (HTTP 429, "You're submitting too quickly. Please wait 15 minutes.")
  1030→2. For self-enrichment: allow the submission but cap XP at 0 for the 4th+ enrichment on the same point by the same user
  1031→3. Escrow XP for all flagged submissions pending review
  1032→
  1033→**Escalation Criteria:**
  1034→- User hits velocity block threshold (15/15min) -> immediate admin notification
  1035→- User has > 20 self-enrichments across all points -> escalate as systematic gaming
  1036→- Leaderboard anomaly: user jumps >10 ranks in 24 hours -> manual review
  1037→
  1038→**Investigation Steps:**
  1039→1. Analyze temporal distribution of user's submissions (histogram by hour/day)
  1040→2. Map enrichment patterns: which points, what fields, what order
  1041→3. Compare user's submission rate to median rate for their cohort
  1042→4. Check if the enrichment data is actually correct (spot-check against ground truth)
  1043→
  1044→**Resolution Options:**
  1045→
  1046→| Action | Criteria |
  1047→|---|---|
  1048→| Rate limit enforcement | Velocity violation, resets after cool-off period |
  1049→| XP cap per point | Self-enrichment detected, max 2 enrichments earn XP per user per point |
  1050→| XP recalculation | Confirmed gaming, recalculate user's XP excluding fraudulent submissions |
  1051→| Leaderboard demotion | Confirmed gaming, manually adjust ranking |
  1052→
  1053→### 4.3 Playbook P-LOC: Location Spoofing Response
  1054→
  1055→**Trigger:** LocationRisk > 60 with specific signals: impossible travel, IP mismatch, EXIF GPS mismatch.
  1056→
  1057→**Immediate Automated Response:**
  1058→1. For impossible travel (>200 km/h): auto-reject with "Location verification failed"
  1059→2. For IP mismatch (datacenter/VPN): flag for review, allow submission but escrow XP
  1060→3. For EXIF-submission GPS mismatch: already handled by current code (HTTP 400 rejection at `api/submissions/index.ts:504`)
  1061→
  1062→**Investigation Steps:**
  1063→1. Plot the user's submission locations on a map with timestamps to visualize travel patterns
  1064→2. Check if the user's device reports mock location capability (Android: `Settings.Secure.ALLOW_MOCK_LOCATION`)
  1065→3. Compare IP ASN history -- consistent residential ASN vs. rotating datacenter ASNs
  1066→4. For EXIF manipulation: check for EXIF software tag inconsistencies (e.g., "Adobe Photoshop" or "exiftool" as the software field)
  1067→
  1068→**Resolution Options:**
  1069→
  1070→| Action | Criteria |
  1071→|---|---|
  1072→| Reject submission | Clear GPS spoofing (impossible travel, >200 km/h) |
  1073→| Flag + escrow | VPN/proxy detected but data may be legitimate |
  1074→| Account review | 3+ location spoofing flags on same account |
  1075→| Ban | Confirmed systematic GPS spoofing (developer tools / mock location) |
  1076→
  1077→### 4.4 Playbook P-PHO: Photo Fraud Response
  1078→
  1079→**Trigger:** PhotoRisk > 60, or photo hash duplicate detected.
  1080→
  1081→**Immediate Automated Response:**
  1082→1. Exact hash duplicate for different point: auto-reject ("This photo has already been used")
  1083→2. Missing all EXIF metadata + not low-end device: flag for review
  1084→3. Screenshot dimensions detected: flag for review with "screenshot_suspected" tag
  1085→
  1086→**Investigation Steps:**
  1087→1. Reverse image search the photo URL (manual step using Google Images or TinEye)
  1088→2. Check image metadata for telltale signs: consistent dimensions across all user submissions (screenshot bot), impossibly high resolution for claimed device, JPEG quality artifacts inconsistent with phone camera
  1089→3. Compare photo content to Google Maps Street View for the declared GPS location
  1090→
  1091→**Resolution Options:**
  1092→
  1093→| Action | Criteria |
  1094→|---|---|
  1095→| Reject | Exact duplicate photo across different POIs |
  1096→| Reject | Confirmed stock photo / internet image (reverse image search hit) |
  1097→| Flag | Screenshot suspected but content plausible |
  1098→| Allow | Low-end device with missing EXIF (Africa-specific accommodation) |
  1099→
  1100→### 4.5 Playbook P-ACC: Account Abuse Response
  1101→
  1102→**Trigger:** Device cross-reference flag (same deviceId on multiple userIds), or bot behavior detected.
  1103→
  1104→**Immediate Automated Response:**
  1105→1. Bot detection (no clientDevice, scripted patterns): block with CAPTCHA challenge
  1106→2. Multi-account detection: flag all linked accounts, suspend newest accounts
  1107→3. Credential sharing: flag account, send security notification to account email
  1108→
  1109→**Investigation Steps:**
  1110→1. Build an account linkage graph: deviceId -> userIds, IP -> userIds, fingerprint -> userIds
  1111→2. For each cluster of linked accounts, determine the "primary" account (oldest, most submissions)
  1112→3. Check if linked accounts are submitting in the same geographic area (shared device at home) or different areas (coordinated fraud)
  1113→4. Review submission quality across all linked accounts
  1114→
  1115→**Resolution Options:**
  1116→
  1117→| Action | Criteria |
  1118→|---|---|
  1119→| Merge accounts | Shared device, appears to be family/legitimate sharing |
  1120→| Suspend alt accounts | Confirmed alt-farming, preserve primary account |
  1121→| Ban all accounts | Coordinated bot operation |
  1122→| CAPTCHA enforcement | Bot suspicion, require human verification |
  1123→
  1124→### 4.6 Playbook P-POI: Data Poisoning Response
  1125→
  1126→**Trigger:** Field plausibility check fails, or conflicting data from multiple sources on same POI.
  1127→
  1128→**Immediate Automated Response:**
  1129→1. Impossible field values (price <= 0, price > 10000 XAF): auto-reject
  1130→2. Conflicting availability data (3+ status flips in 24h): flag all recent updates for review
  1131→3. Implausible values (price outside market range): accept but escrow, flag for review

  1133→**Investigation Steps:**
  1134→1. Compare the submitted data against other recent submissions for the same POI (consensus analysis)
  1135→2. Check if the submitter has a pattern of negative availability reports for specific businesses
  1136→3. Cross-reference with external data sources (Google Maps, OpenStreetMap) where available
  1137→4. For price data: compare against historical price trends for the same POI
  1138→
  1139→**Resolution Options:**
  1140→
  1141→| Action | Criteria |
  1142→|---|---|
  1143→| Reject field value | Impossible value (price <= 0) |
  1144→| Revert to previous value | Confirmed poisoning, restore last known good data |
  1145→| Flag user | Pattern of intentionally wrong data |
  1146→| Competitor sabotage investigation | User consistently marks specific businesses as closed |

  1150→## 5. False Positive Minimization
  1151→
  1152→### 5.1 Legitimate Scenarios That Resemble Fraud
  1153→
  1154→The African mobile context produces many false positive signals that would be definitive fraud indicators in Western markets. Each must be handled carefully.
  1155→
  1156→#### FP-01: New Users Look Like Fraudsters
  1157→
  1158→**The problem:** A brand-new contributor has no submission history, no trust score, a fresh deviceId, and may submit several POIs on their first field trip. Every risk signal fires: UserRisk is high (new account), TemporalRisk is elevated (multiple submissions in quick succession), BehavioralRisk is elevated (no prior pattern).
  1159→
  1160→**Mitigation:**
  1161→- New User Grace Period: First 3 submissions from a new account receive a 15-point risk score reduction (equivalent to the new-account penalty being waived for the very first submissions)
  1162→- Onboarding flow: guide new users through their first submission with explicit instructions, producing a "tutorial" submission that seeds their trust profile
  1163→- Allow up to 5 submissions in the first hour without velocity flags (one-time allowance)
  1164→
  1165→#### FP-02: Poor GPS Accuracy on Feature Phones
  1166→
  1167→**The problem:** Low-end Android phones (Itel, Infinix Smart, Tecno Pop) common in Cameroon have GPS accuracy of 50-100 meters rather than 5-10 meters. This means the GPS-to-EXIF distance could exceed the 1km threshold even for a legitimate submission if the phone provides coordinates from cell tower triangulation rather than true GPS.
  1168→
  1169→**Mitigation:**
  1170→- The current `detectLowEndFromHints` function in `lib/client/deviceProfile.ts:74-88` already identifies these devices (itel, android go, go edition, infinix smart, memory <= 2GB)
  1171→- When `clientDevice.isLowEnd === true`, increase the submission-to-EXIF GPS match threshold from 1km to 2km
  1172→- Weight the LocationRisk score component by 0.7x for low-end devices (reduce its contribution to the composite score)
  1173→- Never auto-reject based solely on GPS mismatch for low-end devices; always flag for review instead
  1174→
  1175→#### FP-03: Shared Devices
  1176→
  1177→**The problem:** In many African households, a single smartphone is shared among family members. Multiple people may use the same device to contribute to ADL. This triggers the multi-account detection rule (same deviceId, different userIds).
  1178→
  1179→**Mitigation:**
  1180→- Flag but do not auto-suspend when 2 accounts share a deviceId
  1181→- Only escalate to suspension at 3+ accounts per device
  1182→- Allow users to declare "shared device" in their profile settings, which adjusts the deviceId-based rules
  1183→- When shared device is declared, apply per-user rate limits instead of per-device limits
  1184→- Track submission patterns: shared device users typically submit at different times of day (different people), while alt-account farmers submit at all hours
  1185→
  1186→#### FP-04: Intermittent Connectivity and Offline Queue Batching
  1187→
  1188→**The problem:** The offline queue in `lib/client/offlineQueue.ts` is designed for intermittent connectivity. A contributor might collect 8 submissions over a 2-hour field trip while offline, then flush them all at once when they find a Wi-Fi hotspot. This looks identical to a rapid-fire bot: 8 submissions arriving within seconds.
  1189→
  1190→**Mitigation:**
  1191→- Track the `createdAt` timestamp from the offline queue item (client-side creation time) separately from the server-side `createdAt`
  1192→- If the submission payload includes a `queuedAt` timestamp (added by the offline queue), use the time gap between `queuedAt` values for velocity analysis instead of server arrival time
  1193→- Require the offline queue to include a sequence number and the total batch size. A batch of 8 that arrives with sequential timestamps spread over 2 hours is treated differently from 8 simultaneous creations
  1194→- Add `offlineBatch: boolean` flag to the submission payload. When true, apply relaxed velocity thresholds (15/batch instead of 5/15min)
  1195→
  1196→```typescript
  1197→// Proposed addition to SubmissionInput type
  1198→interface SubmissionInput {
  1199→  // ... existing fields
  1200→  queuedAt?: string;         // ISO timestamp from offline queue creation
  1201→  offlineBatchId?: string;   // Identifies submissions from the same offline session
  1202→  offlineBatchSize?: number; // Total items in this batch
  1203→}
  1204→```
  1205→
  1206→#### FP-05: Legitimate Dense POI Areas
  1207→
  1208→**The problem:** In Douala's commercial areas (Marche Bonamoussadi, Carrefour Ange Raphael), multiple mobile money kiosks can be within 10 meters of each other. The duplicate POI detection rule (R-PROX-01) would incorrectly flag these as duplicates.
  1209→
  1210→**Mitigation:**
  1211→- Category-specific proximity thresholds:
  1212→  - `mobile_money`: 10m duplicate threshold (kiosks cluster densely)
  1213→  - `pharmacy`: 50m duplicate threshold (rarely adjacent)
  1214→  - `fuel_station`: 100m duplicate threshold (large footprint, never adjacent)
  1215→- Require name differentiation for close-proximity same-category POIs: two mobile money kiosks within 20m must have different provider names
  1216→- Use photo comparison: if two nearby POIs have visually distinct photos, they are likely genuinely different businesses
  1217→
  1218→#### FP-06: Timestamp Anomalies from Incorrect Phone Clocks
  1219→
  1220→**The problem:** Many feature phones in Africa have incorrect system clocks (never set, battery removed and reset, timezone misconfigured). EXIF timestamps may be hours or years off from reality.
  1221→
  1222→**Mitigation:**
  1223→- Do not use EXIF timestamp alone as a fraud signal
  1224→- Compare EXIF timestamp against server receipt time. If the EXIF timestamp is in the future or more than 24 hours in the past, treat it as "unreliable" rather than "fraudulent"
  1225→- For low-end devices, weight the temporal component of the EXIF trust score at 0.5x
  1226→
  1227→### 5.2 Calibration Using Labeled Data
  1228→
  1229→**Data Collection Process:**
  1230→
  1231→1. Random sampling: each week, an admin reviews 50 randomly selected submissions
  1232→2. For each submission, the admin labels it: `legitimate`, `suspicious`, `confirmed_fraud`
  1233→3. Labels are stored in a `submission_labels` table linked to `event_id`
  1234→4. After 4 weeks (200 labeled samples), run the first calibration analysis
  1235→
  1236→**Calibration Metrics:**
  1237→
  1238→```
  1239→Precision = TruePositives / (TruePositives + FalsePositives)
  1240→Recall    = TruePositives / (TruePositives + FalseNegatives)
  1241→F1        = 2 * Precision * Recall / (Precision + Recall)
  1242→```
  1243→
  1244→**Target:** Precision >= 0.90, Recall >= 0.70. We strongly prefer not falsely accusing legitimate contributors (high precision) even at the cost of missing some fraud (lower recall).
  1245→
  1246→### 5.3 User Friction Tradeoffs
  1247→
  1248→| Friction Level | Mechanism | When Applied | Impact on Conversion |
  1249→|---|---|---|---|
  1250→| Zero | No additional checks beyond current validation | Risk score 0-25, trusted/elite users | None |
  1251→| Low | Flag for async review, user not aware | Risk score 26-50, standard users | None |
  1252→| Medium | "Verifying submission..." delay (2-3 seconds) | Risk score 51-75 | Minimal (perceived as upload time) |
  1253→| High | Request second photo or additional details | Risk score 76+, or specific rule triggers | 10-20% drop-off estimated |
  1254→| Maximum | CAPTCHA + identity verification | Bot detection, suspended accounts | 50%+ drop-off (intentional for bots) |
  1255→
  1256→**Principle:** For legitimate contributors on low-end devices with poor connectivity, every additional second of friction risks losing the submission entirely (timeout, page refresh, battery death). Fraud detection must be as invisible as possible for the bottom 80% of submissions.
  1257→
  1258→### 5.4 Whitelist / Trust Mechanisms
  1259→
  1260→**Organizational Whitelists:**
  1261→- Partner organizations (NGOs, health authorities) that deploy field agents can register their agents for elevated trust. These agents start at trust tier "trusted" instead of "new."
  1262→- Whitelist is managed by admin via a `trusted_organizations` table with a list of pre-approved email domains.
  1263→
  1264→**Geographic Trust Zones:**
  1265→- Areas where ADL has ground-truth data (e.g., locations personally verified by the founding team in Bonamoussadi) receive lighter scrutiny for enrichments.
  1266→- Seed data from `shared/bonamoussadiSeedEvents.ts` (OSM imports) establishes ground-truth anchors. Submissions that enrich these known-good points get a 10-point risk reduction.
  1267→
  1268→**Graduated Trust:**
  1269→- Users earn "verified field agent" badge after 50 approved submissions with trust score >= 80
  1270→- Verified agents can attest to other users' submissions (peer review mechanism)
  1271→- Top 10 leaderboard users receive quarterly manual review of a random sample of their submissions to ensure ongoing quality

  1363→### 6.4 Weekly Fraud Review Process
  1364→
  1365→**Schedule:** Every Monday, 10:00 WAT (West Africa Time).
  1366→
  1367→**Attendees:** Admin team (minimum 1 admin, ideally 2 for cross-validation).
  1368→
  1369→**Agenda:**
  1370→
  1371→1. **Metrics Review (10 min)**
  1372→   - Review all KPIs from section 6.1
  1373→   - Compare to previous week and trailing 4-week average
  1374→   - Flag any metric that has moved > 20% from baseline
  1375→
  1376→2. **Flagged Submission Review (20 min)**
  1377→   - Review all submissions currently in the review queue (risk score 26-75)
  1378→   - For each: approve, reject, or escalate
