    23→
    24→This document builds on the work of Teammates 1-7 and the existing codebase:
    25→
    26→- **Teammate 1 (Local Context):** The 7 verticals (pharmacy, fuel, mobile money, alcohol, billboard, transport road, census proxy), Bonamoussadi's middle-class character, ~560 businesses, seasonal flooding (June-October), and the dominance of the informal sector all shape the UI requirements. Mobile network coverage is strong (MTN, Orange), supporting a PWA approach.
    27→- **Teammate 2 (System Design):** The event-sourced data model (`point_events` with `CREATE_EVENT` / `ENRICH_EVENT`), projected points, weekly snapshots, delta computation, and per-vertical field schemas define exactly what the UI must capture. The `enrichableFields` and `createRequiredFields` per vertical directly inform form design.
    28→- **Teammate 6 (Anti-Fraud):** GPS integrity checks, EXIF validation, photo deduplication, velocity analysis, and confidence scoring all have UI implications. The agent must see fraud check results, trust scores, and quality indicators. Client-side mock location detection and sensor data collection happen transparently during capture.
    29→- **Teammate 7 (Marketing):** The three user personas (field agent, admin reviewer, data consumer/client) and the commercial value of delta reports shape the dashboard designs for each audience.
    30→- **Existing Codebase:** The app is built as a React PWA (Vite, not Next.js) with Tailwind CSS, using lucide-react icons. Current screens include: Splash, Home (map + list toggle), Details, ContributionFlow, Profile, Analytics, Settings, QualityInfo, RewardsCatalog, AdminQueue, and DeltaDashboard. Navigation uses a 4-tab bottom bar (Explore, Contribute, Leaderboard/Impact, Profile). The brand colors are navy `#0f2b46`, gold `#f4c317`, terracotta `#c86b4a`, and forest green `#4c7c59`. Bilingual support (EN/FR) is implemented throughout.

    50→│  Language:      French primary, some English         │
    51→│  Daily Routine:                                     │
    52→│    06:30  Wake up, check assignments on phone       │

    59→│    17:30  Travel home                               │

    62→│  Monthly Income: 80,000-150,000 XAF ($130-250 USD) │
    63→└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
    64→```
    65→
    66→**Key Pain Points:**
    67→1. **Bright sunlight** -- Cannot read screen between 10am-3pm under direct equatorial sun. Needs maximum contrast.
    68→2. **One-handed use** -- Often carrying a bag, umbrella (rainy season), or holding onto a moto-taxi. Needs thumb-reachable controls.
    69→3. **Intermittent connectivity** -- Even in well-connected Bonamoussadi, signal drops in alleys, inside buildings, during rain. Must never lose data.
    70→4. **Battery anxiety** -- Mid-range phones last 6-8 hours with GPS + camera active. Agents fear losing a day's work to a dead battery.
    71→5. **Repetitive data entry fatigue** -- Capturing 50+ POIs per day with similar fields leads to frustration and shortcuts.
    72→6. **French accent characters** -- Needs proper support for e, e with accents, c-cedilla, etc. on standard Android keyboards.
    73→7. **Heat and sweat** -- Touchscreens become less responsive with wet/sweaty fingers. Needs larger touch targets.
    74→
    75→**Accessibility Requirements:**
    76→- Minimum 48dp touch targets (Android Material Design guideline; 44pt for iOS)
    77→- Minimum 16sp body text, 14sp minimum for secondary text
    78→- WCAG AA contrast ratio (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)
    79→- Screen brightness auto-adjustment support
    80→- Haptic feedback on successful actions
    81→- Audio confirmation tones (optional, for eyes-free operation)

   149→## 2. Design Principles & System
   150→
   151→### 2.1 Core Design Principles
   152→
   153→| # | Principle | Rationale |
   154→|---|---|---|
   155→| 1 | **Offline-first, always** | Connectivity in Douala is unreliable. Every interaction must work without a network connection. Sync status must be permanently visible. |
   156→| 2 | **3-tap capture** | The most common action (adding a new POI) should complete in 3 taps: Select Vertical > Take Photo > Confirm. All other fields use smart defaults. |
   157→| 3 | **Thumb-zone priority** | Critical actions (capture button, submit, confirm) must be in the bottom 60% of the screen, within thumb reach on a 6" phone held one-handed. |
   158→| 4 | **Sunlight-readable** | Use high-contrast color combinations (dark text on light backgrounds, or white text on dark backgrounds). Avoid mid-tone grays for critical information. |
   159→| 5 | **Progressive disclosure** | Show only essential fields first. Advanced/optional fields appear on expansion. Reduce cognitive load for low-literacy agents. |
   160→| 6 | **Visual over textual** | Use icons, color codes, and photos as primary information carriers. Text is secondary. Supports low-literacy and bilingual usage. |
   161→| 7 | **Forgiving interactions** | All destructive actions require confirmation. No data loss on accidental back-navigation. Undo available for recent actions. |
   162→| 8 | **Honest feedback** | Never hide sync status, errors, or fraud flags. Trust is built through transparency with agents about their data quality. |

   289→### 2.5 Spacing & Touch Targets
   290→
   291→```
   292→SPACING SCALE (Tailwind-aligned)
   293→────────────────────────────────
   294→4px   (p-1)   -- Icon padding, tight spacing
   295→8px   (p-2)   -- Inner card padding, icon margins
   296→12px  (p-3)   -- Field spacing within forms
   297→16px  (p-4)   -- Card padding, section spacing
   298→24px  (p-6)   -- Section separation
   299→32px  (p-8)   -- Major section breaks
   300→48px  (p-12)  -- Bottom safe area spacing
   301→
   302→TOUCH TARGETS
   303→─────────────
   304→Minimum:     48x48dp (12x12 Tailwind = h-12 w-12)
   305→Preferred:   56x56dp (14x14 Tailwind = h-14 w-14)
   306→CTA Buttons: 56dp height minimum (h-14)
   307→Nav Items:   64dp height (h-16, matching current nav)
   308→Form Fields: 44dp minimum height (h-11)
   309→
   310→SPACING BETWEEN TOUCH TARGETS
   311→──────────────────────────────
   312→Minimum gap: 8dp between adjacent touchable elements
   313→Preferred:   12dp gap for form fields
   314→```
   315→
   316→**Rationale for 48dp minimum:** Standard Android Material Design recommends 48dp. In Douala's humid climate (80-95% humidity year-round), sweaty fingers reduce touch precision. Field agents may be walking while tapping. The 48dp minimum compensates for these conditions.

   318→### 2.6 Dark/Light Mode Strategy
   319→
   320→**Recommendation: Do NOT implement dark mode for v1.**
   321→
   322→Rationale:
   323→1. **Sunlight readability is the priority.** Dark mode is harder to read in bright sunlight. The primary use case is outdoor field capture between 7am-5pm.
   324→2. **Cognitive consistency.** Agents switch between the app and their camera frequently. Maintaining one consistent visual mode reduces disorientation.
   325→3. **Development cost.** Dark mode doubles the testing surface for contrast ratios, color accessibility, and visual hierarchy.
   326→
   327→**Instead, implement:**
   328→- **Auto-brightness support:** Ensure the app does not override system brightness settings
   329→- **High-contrast mode:** A settings toggle that increases all text to bold weight and switches to maximum-contrast color pairs (pure black on white)
   330→- **Night capture mode (future):** For alcohol outlet agents working evening shifts, a true dark theme could be added later when the use case is validated

   781→OPTIMAL CAPTURE SEQUENCE (Target: 30-45 seconds per POI)
   782→=========================================================
   783→
   784→1. APPROACH (0s)
   785→   Agent walks up to a pharmacy.
   786→   Phone is in hand, app is on Home/Map screen.
   787→   The map shows their blue dot near an existing POI marker.
   788→
   789→2. DECISION POINT (5s)
   790→   Two paths:
   791→
   792→   Path A: EXISTING POI (tap marker on map)
   793→   ├── Popup shows: "Pharmacie de Bonamoussadi"
   794→   ├── Popup shows: "Last updated 3 days ago"
   795→   ├── Popup shows: "Gaps: isOnDuty, openingHours"

   798→       ├── Agent takes photo (1 tap)
   799→       ├── Form shows ONLY the 2 gap fields
   800→       ├── Agent fills: isOnDuty = Oui (1 tap)
   801→       ├── Agent fills: openingHours = "08:00-20:00" (3 taps)
   802→       └── Agent taps [Soumettre] (1 tap)

   804→
   805→   Path B: NEW POI (tap floating + button)
   806→   ├── Grid shows 7 verticals
   807→   ├── Agent taps "Pharmacie" (1 tap)
   808→   ├── Camera opens with overlay guide
   809→   ├── Agent takes photo (1 tap)
   810→   ├── Form shows:
   811→   │   - Name (auto-filled from nearby data? Or voice input)
   812→   │   - isOpenNow: [Oui] / [Non] (1 tap)
   813→   │   - "+ More fields" collapsed
   814→   ├── Agent taps [Suivant] (1 tap)

   824→### 4.2 Progressive Disclosure Strategy
   825→
   826→Each vertical has `createRequiredFields` and `enrichableFields` defined in `shared/verticals.ts`. The UI uses this to determine what to show:
   827→
   828→```
   829→PROGRESSIVE DISCLOSURE LEVELS
   830→──────────────────────────────
   831→
   832→Level 1: MANDATORY (always visible)
   833→  - Photo (camera step)
   834→  - GPS (auto-captured)
   835→  - createRequiredFields for the vertical
   836→
   837→  Example for Pharmacy:
   838→    - name (text input)
   839→    - isOpenNow (binary toggle)
   840→
   841→Level 2: RECOMMENDED (collapsed, expandable)
   842→  - enrichableFields minus createRequiredFields
   843→  - Shown as "+ X more fields (optional)"
   844→
   845→  Example for Pharmacy:
   846→    - openingHours
   847→    - isOnDuty
   848→    - isLicensed
   849→    - hasPrescriptionService
   850→    - medicineCategories
   851→
   852→Level 3: ADVANCED (hidden, accessible from settings)
   853→  - Secondary photo
   854→  - Notes / free text
   855→  - Override GPS coordinates
   856→  - Device diagnostics
   857→```
   858→
   859→**Implementation rule:** Level 1 fields use full-width, high-contrast input components. Level 2 fields appear in a collapsible section with a muted background. Level 3 fields are accessible only through a "More options" menu.
   860→
   861→### 4.3 Smart Defaults and Auto-Fill
   862→
   863→| Auto-Fill Source | What It Fills | How |
   864→|---|---|---|
   865→| **GPS coordinates** | Location fields | `navigator.geolocation` with high accuracy mode |
   866→| **Nearby POI names** | Name field suggestion | Query existing points within 50m radius; suggest as autocomplete |
   867→| **Previous submission** | Vertical selection | Default to the last-used vertical (if agent is doing a sweep of one type) |
   868→| **Time of day** | isOpenNow | Default to "Yes" during business hours (8am-6pm), "No" otherwise |
   869→| **EXIF data** | Photo GPS, timestamp | Extracted by `exifr` library (already in codebase) |
   870→| **Device sensor** | Mock location flag, accelerometer | Collected via `gpsIntegrity.ts` (from Teammate 6's design) |
   871→| **OSM seed data** | Name, brand, operator | Cross-reference GPS against `external_id` from OSM imports |

  1439→## 8. Benchmark Research
  1440→
  1441→### 8.1 ODK Collect / KoboToolbox
  1442→
  1443→**What works:**
  1444→- **Offline-first architecture**: Both ODK Collect and KoboCollect are designed for zero-connectivity environments. Forms are downloaded once and work entirely offline. ADL's current offline queue (`offlineQueue.ts`) follows this pattern correctly.
  1445→- **Skip logic and validation**: KoboToolbox supports cascading selections and conditional field display. ADL should adopt this for vertical-specific fields (e.g., show `fuelTypes` only when vertical is `fuel_station`).
  1446→- **GPS auto-capture**: Both tools auto-capture GPS at form start, with accuracy indicators. ADL already does this.
  1447→
  1448→**What does not work:**
  1449→- **Form-centric UX**: ODK Collect is designed around filling out a survey form, not capturing a real-world POI. The "form" metaphor creates cognitive overhead for field agents who think in terms of "places" not "records."
  1450→- **No visual feedback**: ODK Collect shows a plain list of saved forms with no map, no gamification, no progress visualization. This leads to agent disengagement.
  1451→- **Desktop-first design**: KoboToolbox's form builder is a desktop web app that does not translate well to mobile review workflows.
  1452→- **No photo overlay guides**: Both tools treat photos as generic attachments, not as structured evidence with composition guides.
  1453→
  1454→**ADL advantage:** The map-first, POI-centric approach is fundamentally better for location data capture than the form-centric approach of ODK/Kobo. The existing Home screen with map + list toggle is the right paradigm.
  1455→
  1456→### 8.2 Mapillary
  1457→
  1458→**What works:**
  1459→- **Continuous capture mode**: Mapillary's auto-capture (every 2 seconds while moving) is the inspiration for ADL's batch capture mode.
  1460→- **Screen dimming during capture**: Saves battery during long capture sessions. ADL should implement this.
  1461→- **Upload queue with progress**: Shows pending uploads, upload progress, and retry controls. ADL's submission queue design follows this pattern.
  1462→- **Distance and coverage tracking**: Shows total distance covered and coverage percentage on a map.
  1463→
  1464→**What does not work:**
  1465→- **Street-level imagery focus**: Mapillary captures street-view imagery, not structured POI data. The camera-only workflow does not support the data fields ADL needs.
  1466→- **No offline map tiles**: Mapillary requires connectivity for map display. ADL must support offline map tiles.
  1467→- **No gamification beyond contribution count**: No quality-weighted scoring, no streaks, no badges.
  1468→
  1469→**ADL advantage:** Mapillary's capture workflow combined with structured data entry (photo + fields) is a stronger approach than either tool alone.
  1470→
  1471→### 8.3 OpenStreetMap (iD Editor, JOSM, StreetComplete)
  1472→
  1473→**What works:**
  1474→- **StreetComplete** (Android app) is the closest benchmark to ADL's field capture:
  1475→  - Shows "quests" on a map (tasks to complete at specific locations)
  1476→  - Simple question-and-answer flow for each quest
  1477→  - Gamification with solved quest counts
  1478→  - Offline-capable
  1479→- **iD Editor**: Simple web-based editing with visual context.
  1480→- **Tag-based data model**: OSM's key-value tags are analogous to ADL's JSONB details.
  1481→
  1482→**What does not work:**
  1483→- **Volunteer-driven quality**: No formal review process, no fraud detection, no incentive alignment.
  1484→- **Technical barrier**: Contributing to OSM requires understanding the data model (nodes, ways, tags). ADL abstracts this with vertical-specific forms.
  1485→- **No photo evidence**: OSM does not require or store photo evidence for edits. ADL's photo-first approach is critical for trust.
  1486→
  1487→**ADL advantage:** StreetComplete's "quest" model mapped to ADL's "assignment" system, combined with photo evidence and fraud detection, creates a more trustworthy data product.
  1488→
  1489→### 8.4 African Tech Design Patterns
  1490→
  1491→**M-Pesa App:**
  1492→- **Simplicity**: The M-Pesa app uses a "less is more" approach. The home screen shows 6 large icons (Send, Withdraw, Pay, Save, Borrow, More). Each is a single action.
  1493→- **Trust through simplicity**: Financial apps in Africa succeed when they minimize decision points. Users trust apps that feel simple and predictable.
  1494→- **Offline resilience**: M-Pesa's USSD fallback (works on feature phones without internet) is a benchmark for reliability expectations.
  1495→- **Green color scheme**: M-Pesa uses Safaricom green. ADL's forest green (`#4c7c59`) serves a similar trust signal.
  1496→
  1497→**Jumia App:**
  1498→- **Performance-first design**: Jumia adopted a "less is more" design philosophy after observing that low-end devices and poor connections caused users to abandon the app. Light pages, compressed images, and step-by-step checkout flows improved conversion.
  1499→- **Step-by-step flows**: Jumia's checkout is divided into steps "not only for performance reasons but also to inform the user of progress." ADL's proposed 4-step capture wizard follows this pattern.
  1500→- **Localized experience**: Support for multiple languages and local payment methods.
  1501→
  1502→**Key takeaways for ADL from African tech:**
  1503→1. **Minimize data usage**: Compress photos before upload. Cache map tiles. Use lazy loading for screens (already implemented with `React.lazy`).
  1504→2. **Step-based flows**: Break complex actions into discrete steps with progress indicators.
  1505→3. **Large touch targets**: African users often use devices with cracked screens or in dusty/humid conditions. Generous touch targets compensate.
  1506→4. **Trust signals**: Show verification badges, sync status, and quality scores prominently. Users in emerging markets are more skeptical of digital tools.
  1507→5. **Bottom navigation**: Both M-Pesa and Jumia use bottom tab bars. ADL already implements this correctly.
