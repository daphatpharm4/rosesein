    33→## 1. Vertical-by-Vertical Matrix
    34→
    35→### 1.1 Billboards
    36→
    37→| Dimension | Detail |
    38→|---|---|
    39→| **Data Needed** | GPS location, format/size, operator/owner, advertiser brand, content category, occupancy status (booked/vacant), condition, photo evidence, installation date |
    40→| **Actors (Producer to Consumer)** | **Regulators:** Ministry of Communication (licensing), Douala City Council (permits). **Operators:** Alliance Media, JC Decaux Cameroun, McCann Douala, Acmar Media, local operators. **Advertisers:** MTN, Orange, SABC/Brasseries du Cameroun, banks, FMCG. **Agencies:** Media buying/planning agencies (Sortlist-listed, Bamboo Agency). **Informal:** Sign painters, poster distributors, small business owners. |
    41→| **Products/Services** | Monthly billboard market report (PDF/dashboard), real-time alert feed for content changes, one-time competitive audit, brand share-of-voice analytics |
    42→| **Likely Problems** | Boundary ambiguity between formal billboards and large commercial signage; ownership attribution (many lack visible operator markings); seasonal damage during rainy season (June-Oct); periodic municipal crackdowns remove unauthorized boards in batches |
    43→| **Data Gaps** | No centralized geolocated billboard inventory for Douala exists; informal signage is completely unmapped; occupancy rates (booked vs. vacant) are not public; no historical lifecycle data |
    44→| **Delta Method** | Monthly field re-survey of all billboard locations. Track: new installations (commercial investment signal), vacancy rate changes (advertising market health), informal-to-formal ratio shifts (regulatory enforcement), content category shifts (industry spending trends) |

    46→### 1.2 Alcohol
    47→
    48→| Dimension | Detail |
    49→|---|---|
    50→| **Data Needed** | GPS location, business name, formal/informal classification, brands available, operating hours, price points, license status, photo evidence |
    51→| **Actors (Producer to Consumer)** | **Producers:** SABC/Brasseries du Cameroun (dominant, 75%+ market), UCB, informal distillers. **Importers:** Formal (wines, spirits), informal cross-border (Nigeria). **Distributors:** SABC network, independent wholesalers, informal. **Retailers:** Licensed bars, restaurants, off-licences, street vendors, informal spots. **Regulators:** Ministry of Commerce (licensing), Ministry of Public Health (quality). **Consumers:** Urban middle class (branded beer), lower income (artisanal brews, sachets). |
    52→| **Products/Services** | Quarterly distribution intelligence report, monthly alert digest (new openings/closures), annual market structure report, enterprise API for distribution management integration |
    53→| **Likely Problems** | High informal turnover (unlicensed spots open/close rapidly); dual-purpose establishments (shops selling alcohol alongside other goods); under-reporting by operators; counterfeit/smuggled products indistinguishable at POS; night-only operations make daytime capture incomplete |
    54→| **Data Gaps** | No geolocated POS database in Douala; informal sector volume estimated but unmeasured; product-level distribution data proprietary to SABC; quality/safety data at POS non-existent; operating hours undocumented |
    55→| **Delta Method** | Weekly agent re-surveys of commercial corridors. Track: new establishment openings/closures (economic vitality), informal-to-formal ratio (regulatory signal), brand distribution shifts (market competition), price point changes (inflation indicator), density changes by micro-zone (commercial development) |

    57→### 1.3 Census (Demographic Proxy)
    58→
    59→| Dimension | Detail |
    60→|---|---|
    61→| **Data Needed** | Building count, building height (stories), construction status (new/in-progress/complete), ground-floor commercial occupancy, residential/commercial classification, building condition, photo evidence |
    62→| **Actors (Producer to Consumer)** | **Official:** BUCREP (census authority, last census 2005), INS (statistical surveys), Ministry of Economy/Planning. **Local:** Douala V Municipality, community chiefs (chefs de quartier). **International:** UNFPA, UNDP, World Bank. **Private:** Real estate developers (SIC, Diamond Realty), banks (Afriland, Ecobank), telecoms (network planning). |
    63→| **Products/Services** | Quarterly neighborhood profile update, annual neighborhood census report, custom demographic analysis per project |
    64→| **Likely Problems** | 21-year-old baseline (2005 census) makes all projections unreliable; rapid urbanization since 2005 (1.9M to 4.35M); informal residents and transient populations systematically missed; no address system in most of Douala; political sensitivity of census data |
    65→| **Data Gaps** | No neighborhood-level population data less than 20 years old; zero building-level occupancy data; no demographic composition at neighborhood level; migration patterns untracked; no household income data at micro-zone level |
    66→| **Delta Method** | Monthly building footprint surveys using field agents + satellite imagery comparison. Track: building construction/demolition (population proxy), occupancy rates, commercial establishment density (economic activity proxy), building height changes (densification indicator) |

    71→|---|---|
    72→| **Data Needed** | GPS location, operator/brand, services offered (convenience store, car wash, mobile money, food), stock-out status, price compliance, informal vendor presence nearby, queue indicators, photo evidence |

    76→| **Data Gaps** | No real-time fuel availability data at station level; queue lengths/wait times untracked; informal sales volume unknown; station-level service inventory not centralized; fuel quality not monitored systematically |
    77→| **Delta Method** | Weekly station visits (5-10 stations are manageable). Track: stock-out events (supply chain health), new station construction (infrastructure investment), informal vendor density near stations (supply constraint indicator), service diversification (market maturation), price compliance |

    91→
    92→| Dimension | Detail |
    93→|---|---|
    94→| **Data Needed** | GPS location, name, formal/informal classification, operating hours, on-duty rotation (pharmacie de garde), product categories available, proximity to health facilities, license status, photo evidence |
    95→| **Actors (Producer to Consumer)** | **Regulators:** DPML (licensing), LANACOME (quality control), IGSPL (inspection), Pharmacists Council. **Supply:** CENAME (national essential medicines procurement). **Distributors:** Licensed pharmaceutical wholesalers. **Formal Retail:** Licensed community pharmacies. **Informal:** Street vendors, market sellers, unregistered medicine shops. **Prescribers:** Doctors, clinics, hospitals. **International:** Sanofi, Novartis, Roche, WHO, UNICEF, USAID. |
    96→| **Products/Services** | Monthly pharmacy access report, quarterly public health intelligence report, pharmacie de garde API for consumer apps, distribution intelligence for pharma companies |

    98→| **Data Gaps** | No public geolocated directory of licensed pharmacies in Douala; informal vendor locations unmapped; real-time drug availability not tracked at retail; operating hours and on-duty schedules not digitized; retail drug pricing not monitored |
    99→| **Delta Method** | Monthly formal pharmacy visits, weekly informal vendor surveys near markets. Track: informal vendor density changes (enforcement/public health signal), new pharmacy openings, stock-out frequency for essential medicines, pharmacy-to-population ratio |

   101→### 1.7 Transport Roads
   102→
   103→| Dimension | Detail |
   104→|---|---|
   105→| **Data Needed** | Road segment GPS coordinates, surface type (paved/unpaved/gravel/laterite), condition rating, blockage status (clear/partial/blocked), cause of blockage (flooding/construction/accident), vehicle passability (sedan/SUV/truck/moto), estimated clearance time, bypass route, photo evidence |
   106→| **Actors (Producer to Consumer)** | **Infrastructure:** Ministry of Public Works (national), Douala City Council (municipal). **Contractors:** Road construction companies (often Chinese-funded). **Transport:** Taxi drivers, moto-taxi operators, bus companies, freight/logistics companies. **Traffic police.** **Emergency services.** **Ride-hailing:** Yango, Gozem. **Development:** World Bank ($543M BRT project, construction 2026). |
   107→| **Products/Services** | Real-time road blockage alert API, weekly road condition dashboard, rainy season flood risk map, construction zone tracking, enterprise routing integration for logistics/ride-hailing |
   108→| **Likely Problems** | Conditions change within hours during rainy season (June-Oct); GPS accuracy reduced in urban canyons; "blocked" is subjective (spectrum from impassable to difficult); seasonal bias in dry-season data; construction zones may lack signage |
   109→| **Data Gaps** | No real-time road condition system for Douala; historical flooding patterns not digitized by street; traffic density data unavailable; blocked roads communicated only via social media/word of mouth; road surface condition not systematically mapped; construction project locations not centrally tracked |
   110→| **Delta Method** | Daily to real-time field reporting during rainy season; weekly during dry season. Track: blockage events (highest real-time value), condition deterioration/improvement over time, new paving or rehabilitation, flooding recurrence by location, construction project progress |

   114→## 2. Recommended Collection Mix Per Vertical
   115→
   116→The percentages below represent the recommended source of initial data for the Bonamoussadi pilot, calibrated to local realities.
   117→
   118→| Source | Billboards | Alcohol | Census | Fuel | Mobile Money | Pharmacy | Transport Roads |
   119→|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
   120→| **Field Ops** (paid agents) | 70% | 80% | 50% | 60% | 75% | 75% | 60% |
   121→| **Satellite Imagery** | 5% | 0% | 25% | 5% | 0% | 0% | 15% |
   122→| **Partner Data** (telcos, fuel cos, operators) | 5% | 5% | 5% | 15% | 10% | 5% | 5% |
   123→| **Public Data** (govt registers, OSM) | 10% | 5% | 15% | 15% | 5% | 15% | 15% |
   124→| **Competitor Tracking** | 10% | 10% | 5% | 5% | 10% | 5% | 5% |
   125→
   126→**Rationale:**
   127→
   128→- **Field ops dominate every vertical** because Bonamoussadi's informal economy (30-50% of GDP) is invisible to satellites, government registers, and partner databases. Ground truth is ADL's core value proposition.
   129→- **Satellite imagery** is most useful for Census (building footprint change detection) and Roads (surface-level condition visible from above). It is useless for identifying whether a kiosk sells mobile money or alcohol.
   130→- **Partner data** is highest for Fuel (branded operators like TotalEnergies and Tradex maintain station lists) and Mobile Money (MTN/Orange have agent directories, though proprietary).
   131→- **Public data** contributes most to Fuel (CSPH price data), Pharmacy (Pharmacists Council registry), Roads (OSM road geometry, Ministry of Public Works classifications), and Census (BUCREP 2005 baseline, however outdated).
   132→- **Competitor tracking** (monitoring what Google Maps, OSM volunteers, and Alliance Media publish) provides baseline cross-references, especially for Billboards and Mobile Money.

   455→
   456→### 7.2 Confidence Scoring Formula
   457→
   458→ADL uses two complementary scores:
   459→
   460→#### A. Data Quality Confidence Score (0-100)
   461→
   462→Measures how reliable a data point is. Computed per POI and updated with each event.
   463→
   464→```
   465→DATA QUALITY CONFIDENCE SCORE
   466→==============================
   467→
   468→Factor                Weight    Scoring
   469→--------------------------------------------------
   470→Recency               25%      100 if <7 days old, decays linearly to 0 at 90 days
   471→Source Count           20%      min(100, sourceCount * 25) -- more sources = higher confidence
   472→Photo Evidence         20%      100 if photo with EXIF GPS; 60 if photo without EXIF; 0 if no photo
   473→GPS Accuracy           15%      100 if <10m; 80 if <30m; 50 if <50m; 20 if <100m; 0 if >100m
   474→Reviewer Approval      10%      100 if admin approved; 50 if pending; 0 if rejected
   475→Field Completeness     10%      (filled fields / total fields) * 100
   476→
   477→FINAL SCORE = weighted sum of all factors, clamped to 0-100
   478→
   479→Publication threshold: >= 40 (below 40, data is withheld from client-facing products)
   480→High-confidence threshold: >= 70 (used for premium data products)
   481→```
   482→
   483→#### B. Fraud Risk Score (0-100)
   484→
   485→Measures how likely a submission is fraudulent. Computed at submission time.
   486→
   487→```
   488→FRAUD RISK SCORE
   489→=================
   490→
   491→Category              Weight    Signals
   492→--------------------------------------------------
   493→GPS Integrity          25%      Inverted GPS confidence (100 - GPS_CONFIDENCE_SCORE)
   494→                                Mock location detection, EXIF mismatch, IP mismatch,
   495→                                velocity analysis, sensor data
   496→
   497→Photo Integrity        25%      SHA-256 exact duplicate check (reject if different POI)
   498→                                Perceptual hash (aHash, Hamming distance <=5 = near-identical)
   499→                                EXIF fingerprint match (same device+time+GPS)
   500→                                Photo freshness (100 if <30 min, decays; <10 if >7 days)
   501→                                Screenshot detection, metadata editing detection
   502→
   503→Temporal Consistency   15%      Submission velocity (>200 km/h = block, >80 km/h = flag)
   504→                                Time-of-day plausibility
   505→                                Offline batch anomaly analysis
   506→
   507→Agent History          20%      Account age, prior fraud flags, prior rejections,
   508→                                trust tier (new/established/veteran),
   509→                                consecutive clean submissions
   510→
   511→Behavioral Patterns    15%      Self-enrichment patterns, device sharing,
   512→                                category distribution anomalies, IP reputation (VPN/datacenter)
   513→
   514→COMPOSITE FRAUD RISK = weighted sum, clamped to 0-100
   515→```
   516→
   517→#### C. Auto-Routing Rules
   518→
   519→| Fraud Risk Score | Data Quality Score | Action | XP |
   520→|---|---|---|---|
   521→| 0-20 | >= 70 | **Auto-approve** | Award immediately |
   522→| 0-20 | 40-69 | **Soft review** (admin reviews within 48h) | Escrow until review |
   523→| 21-50 | Any | **Soft review** | Escrow until review |
   524→| 51-70 | Any | **Hard review** (admin must investigate) | Deny until cleared |
   525→| 71-100 | Any | **Auto-reject** | Deny |

   527→### 7.3 Fraud Detection Checkpoints
   528→
   529→Each submission passes through these checkpoints in order:
   530→
   531→```
   532→CHECKPOINT 1: CLIENT-SIDE (before submission)
   533→  [ ] Mock location API check (Android isMocked flag)
   534→  [ ] GPS accuracy sanity (reject if accuracy = 0 or <1m on consumer device)
   535→  [ ] Sensor data collection (2 sec accelerometer sample)
   536→  [ ] Network type recording
   537→  [ ] GPS timestamp vs device clock check (flag if >30s drift)
   538→
   539→CHECKPOINT 2: SERVER-SIDE (at submission time)
   540→  [ ] EXIF GPS vs submission GPS (threshold: 1km standard, 2km low-end device)
   541→  [ ] IP geolocation vs submission GPS (threshold: 50km)
   542→  [ ] Velocity check (time + distance since last submission by same user)
   543→      - >200 km/h or >5km in <2 min = BLOCK
   544→      - >80 km/h = FLAG
   545→  [ ] Photo SHA-256 exact hash check against all prior submissions
   546→  [ ] Perceptual hash check (Hamming distance <=5 on aHash = flag)
   547→  [ ] EXIF fingerprint dedup (same device + time + GPS = flag)
   548→  [ ] Photo freshness check (>7 days old = reject; >24h = flag)
   549→  [ ] Screenshot detection (no EXIF + specific resolution patterns)
   550→  [ ] Entity proximity dedup (per-vertical thresholds, e.g., 10m for mobile money)
   551→  [ ] Geofence check (within Bonamoussadi: 4.0755-4.0999 N, 9.7185-9.7602 E)
   552→
   553→CHECKPOINT 3: BATCH ANALYSIS (daily cron)
   554→  [ ] Agent movement pattern analysis (cluster count, radius, precision distribution)
   555→  [ ] Cross-agent duplicate detection (different agents submitting same entity)
   556→  [ ] Collusion pattern detection (circular enrichment graphs)
   557→  [ ] Coordinate precision anomaly (>80% of submissions with <=4 decimal places = flag)
   558→  [ ] Alt account detection (same deviceId or IP across multiple userIds)
   559→```

   561→### 7.4 Review Workflow
   562→
   563→```
   564→ADMIN REVIEW QUEUE WORKFLOW
   565→=============================
   566→
   567→1. TRIAGE (daily, 9:00 AM WAT)
   568→   - Sort queue by fraud risk score (highest first)
   569→   - Auto-rejected items: log and archive (no action needed)
   570→   - Auto-approved items: log and publish (no action needed)

   572→   - Soft review items: batch review after hard reviews

   581→
   582→3. DECISION
   583→   - APPROVE: submission enters published dataset; XP released to agent
   584→   - REJECT: submission archived; XP denied; agent notified with reason
   585→   - REQUEST RE-SUBMISSION: agent asked to revisit and re-capture
   586→   - MERGE: duplicate submission merged with existing POI (enrichment)
   587→
   588→4. AGENT FEEDBACK
   589→   - Rejection reason communicated to agent via app notification
   590→   - Repeated rejections trigger trust score reduction
   591→   - 3+ confirmed fraud incidents = agent suspension pending investigation

   594→### 7.5 Spot-Check Audit Protocol
   595→
   596→Physical field verification of data accuracy:
   597→
   598→```
   599→SPOT-CHECK AUDIT SOP
   600→=====================
   601→
   602→Frequency: Weekly (W3-W6), expanding scope each week
   603→  - Week 3: 10 random POIs
   604→  - Week 4: 10 different random POIs
   605→  - Week 5: 15 random POIs
   606→  - Week 6: 50 POIs (comprehensive, all verticals)
   607→
   608→Executor: Field Team Lead (independent of agents who submitted)
   609→
   610→For each audited POI:
   611→  1. Navigate to the GPS coordinates recorded in the submission
   612→  2. Verify EXISTENCE: Does the entity physically exist at this location? (Y/N)
   613→  3. Verify NAME: Does the name match what was submitted? (Y/N/Close)
   614→  4. Verify CATEGORY: Is the vertical classification correct? (Y/N)
   615→  5. Verify STATUS: Is the operating status correct (open/closed)? (Y/N)
   616→  6. Verify PHOTO: Does the submission photo match what you see? (Y/N)
   617→  7. Take independent verification photo
   618→  8. Note any discrepancies
   619→
   620→Scoring:
   621→  - MATCH: All 5 criteria verified (existence, name, category, status, photo)
   622→  - PARTIAL: 3-4 criteria match (acceptable, may need enrichment)
   623→  - MISMATCH: <3 criteria match (data quality failure)
   624→
   625→Audit accuracy = (MATCH + PARTIAL) / Total Audited * 100
   626→
   627→Threshold: >= 80% (W3), >= 85% (W4-W5), >= 85% (W6 comprehensive)
   628→
   629→If audit accuracy drops below 70%: PAUSE collection; retrain agents; investigate cause
   630→```
