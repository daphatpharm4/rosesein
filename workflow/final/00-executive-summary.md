# ROSE-SEIN Workflow Executive Summary

ROSE-SEIN already has the beginnings of a coherent platform: a canonical Next.js shell, a working magic-link auth foundation, protected route boundaries, and a first Supabase schema with RLS. What it does not yet have is a usable MVP. Most value-critical surfaces are still scaffolded, mock-backed, or operationally undefined.

The best near-term strategy is narrow and deliberate. Keep the public website as the institutional and educational trust layer. Use the app as the private support layer. Build only what the association can safely operate: account onboarding, validated content, events, association-first messaging, and settings. Defer open community, secure document upload, and broader social mechanics until moderation, auditability, and role-aware operations are in place.

The product should be framed as trusted digital support infrastructure for association-led accompaniment. That positioning is stronger than “health app” and safer than “social community.” The right MVP proves that ROSE-SEIN can convert public trust into moderated private utility.
